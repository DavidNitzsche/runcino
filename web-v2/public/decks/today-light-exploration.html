What if
it’s not dark?

The current /today surface is shipped in a dark canvas, and it's beautiful — but the gut says try lighter. Same layout, same gradients, same typography. Only the canvas + cards change. Four palette directions, each rendering the full /today system so the comparison is honest: poster, sibling, week strip, coach voice, body grid. The Poster gradient is sacred and unchanged. The question is what lives behind it.

DIRECTION A · CREAM

Warm paper.

Off-white #f6f1e7 canvas. Cream cards. Warm-grey hairlines. The gradients pop with more saturation against the warm field than they ever do against black. Feels like a Moleskine spread that knows your splits. Reads serious, hand-built, considered — not clinical.

Sprint 03.

The four I picked.

Four directions chosen because each makes a fundamentally different argument about what the canvas does to the gradients. Cream warms them, Pearl cleans them, Glass amplifies them across the whole system, Hybrid frames them like a museum object. Two skipped because they didn't add a distinct argument.

PICKED · A · CREAM
The warmth angle. Off-white paper canvas. Distinct from B because Pearl is cold-clinical and Cream is warm-considered — very different emotional reads. The gradients gain saturation against the warm field.
PICKED · B · PEARL
The clinical angle. Pure cool white with shadow lift, no hairlines. The Strava / Apple Fitness territory — instantly familiar to runners. Tests whether Faff wants to look like a friend or like a tool.
PICKED · D · GLASS
The maximalist gradient angle. The brief asked specifically to lean MORE into gradients on the other cards — this direction does exactly that. Per-tile and per-day stripes turn gradient into a system-wide language, not just a Poster property.
PICKED · E · HYBRID
The "what if we’re only half wrong" angle. Maybe the dark canvas is right but the dark cards are the problem. Inverse approach — keep the dramatic page, swap to bright cards. Highest contrast of any direction.
SKIPPED · C · LIGHT GREY DASHBOARD
Too close to B. Notion-grey canvas with white cards lives in the same "app dashboard" emotional register as Pearl, but Pearl earns its keep with the shadow-lift language. Light-Grey-Dashboard would mostly demonstrate the same argument B already makes.
SKIPPED · F · GRADIENT-EVERY-CARD
The brief flagged it as "probably bad." Gradient on every card would gut the Poster's role as the loud object — if everything shouts, nothing carries the day-state identity. Glass already explores gradient-as-system more thoughtfully (accent bands, not full backgrounds).

My read.

Cream is the one. The gradients are the brand soul, and they pop hardest against the warm paper canvas — more saturated, more present than they ever look against #0a0c10. The cream cards also do something Pearl can't: they feel built by a person, not a platform. Faff's voice in the Coach card is hand-built and considered, and Cream is the only canvas that reads the same way. Glass is the most fun and the most likely to be a marketing screenshot, but it's also the easiest to over-cook — the gradient-band-per-day-card detail is gorgeous in this mockup and would need real discipline to keep tasteful at production scale. Hybrid is a tempting middle path, but light-on-dark fights itself on the WeekStrip (cream cards on black makes the type cards feel like flashcards floating in a void). Pearl is correct and forgettable. Cream is correct and felt.

Open questions.

— Does Cream survive when the day-state is RACE or QUALITY? The warm canvas might fight the warm-orange Race gradient — needs a second pass at race-week.
— In Direction D the per-day-card gradient strips are subtle here (3px at 55%) — should they be louder? Or should they only appear on the today-card and the upcoming long?
— For Hybrid: does the page background stay #0a0c10 or shift to a warmer near-black to harmonise with the cream cards (e.g. #14110c)?
