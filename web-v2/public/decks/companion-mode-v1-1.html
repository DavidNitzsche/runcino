FAFF · COMPANION MODE · v1.1+ RESEARCH · 2026-05-28

When the
plan isn't
yours.

Faff v1 ships to plan-following runners without an external human coach. Lilian's path. Tyler's path forks earlier and stays forked — he already has a coach programming his weeks in Final Surge or TrainingPeaks. Companion Mode (locked v1.1+) is Faff acknowledging someone else owns the plan and staying in its lane: body, recovery, race horizon, watch, race-day ops.

Same Faff design system as /today and onboarding-lilian. Dark canvas, Oswald 700 for hero questions, Inter for body, the existing gradient palette. No new colors. Mockups at iPhone width (390 × 760). Research deck — not ship-ready.

SECTION 1 · THE TYLER PERSONA

Has a coach.
Wants Faff anyway.

Tyler is a sub-2:50 marathoner whose weekly program is written by a human coach he pays $200/mo. The schedule lands in Final Surge every Sunday night. He still wants Faff — for the body dashboard, the race horizon, the watch app, the race-day Uber + checklist. He does not want Faff generating plans, second-guessing his coach, or quietly adjusting his Tuesday intervals when his HRV is low.

TY
Tyler · 34 · NYC

Coached.
Still chooses Faff.

Has had the same human coach for three years. Coach uses Final Surge for everything. Tyler doesn't want to manage two plans. He wants Faff for the things his coach + Final Surge don't do well — body context (Whoop killer), race-day operations (Uber, checklist, transport), the Apple Watch experience, and a clean daily surface that doesn't pretend to know better than his coach.

HAS
Live human coach · Final Surge subscription · Garmin watch · Strava
WANTS
Body dashboard · race horizon · watch face · race-day ops · clean daily surface
DOES NOT WANT
Plan generation · workout autocorrect · pace-band overrides · LTHR re-derivation that conflicts with his coach's zones
Why this persona matters: in interviews + public running communities, ~25-35% of serious goal-race runners use an external coach. Without Companion Mode, Faff is invisible to that segment — they bounce in onboarding the moment "race date → we'll build the plan" appears. Companion Mode is not a feature, it's a path through the same product — same Poster, same Sibling, same WeekStrip, different data source.

The five changes:
1 · Eyebrow reads "TUE · MAY 26 · COACH AARON" instead of "TUE · MAY 26 · BASE" — coach replaces phase as the anchoring identity.
2 · Phase tag replaced by a pill badge "From Coach Aaron · Final Surge" with a live green dot signaling the sync is healthy. Tap → /plan plan-source card.

4 · Sibling coach voice opens with attribution ("Coach Aaron has you on…") before the body read. Never suggests modifying the workout. Surfaces body signal as data, not instruction. The escape hatch is "message Aaron," not "I'll pull the splits."
5 · Stat trio · LTHR BPM is Faff's auto-derived LTHR (the body engine still owns this — it's a Faff-side health number, not a plan number). If Tyler's coach prescribes HR zones inline in the Final Surge workout, those override.

When the ICS feed fails — degrade honestly
Phase strip logic: if the coach's Final Surge plan has named blocks ("Base · Build · Peak · Taper · Race"), Faff shows them verbatim. If the coach uses week-by-week labels with no block tags, Faff renders generic "BLOCK 1 OF 5 · WEEK 6 OF 24" using the race-date math + total weeks. If neither is available, the strip collapses to a single "FROM COACH AARON" tile + week countdown.

Volume arc: built from rolling 28-day completed volume + projection from the next 2 weeks of Final Surge data (no Daniels VDOT recomputation, no ACWR-driven adjustment). Companion Mode does not surface the body engine's volume opinions on /plan — those live exclusively in the Sibling on /today, where they read as observation, not prescription.

SECTION 6 · DATA CONTRACT

Where each
field comes from.

The contract for v1 → v1.1. The right-hand column locks what Companion Mode pulls externally vs what stays Faff-owned. Faff-owned (body signals, race horizon) doesn't change — those are Faff's job whether you have a coach or not. Plan-owned (today's workout, phase label, volume) shifts to the external source. Plan modifications are explicitly never Faff's in Companion Mode.

SECTION 7 · v1.1 vs v1.2+ SCOPE

Where the line
sits, honestly.

v1.1 ships Companion Mode for the Final Surge + manual paths · which covers ~80% of the coached-runner segment with low integration risk. TrainingPeaks OAuth, automatic plan-conflict detection, plan-import history surface — all v1.2+. The line is drawn at: does shipping this require an external API partnership we don't yet have? If yes, push it.

v1.1 · NEXT

Ships in the
Companion Mode round.

  • Final Surge ICS calendar import — read-only feed URL, parsed for structured workout, 6h sync interval + on-launch refresh.
  • Manual workout-log path — for runners with non-Final-Surge coaches (Excel, email, Notion). Tyler logs the day's workout in plain prose; Faff treats it as the source of truth without inferring more.
  • Step 1 fork + Step 2.5 plan-source onboarding — the modified flow shown in Section 2.
  • /today Companion variant — coach attribution badge, verbatim workout detail, observational Sibling, "message your coach" Skip Today.
  • /plan Companion variant — external phase strip, history-projected volume arc, plan-source card with sync state.
  • Stale-sync degradation — eyebrow + source card surface the failure honestly, last-known workout marked stale.
  • Coach name + plan source on /me — single editable field, "Change plan owner" routes back to Step 1 flow.
v1.2+ · LATER

Waits until the
v1.1 baseline lands.

  • TrainingPeaks OAuth integration — pending API partner status. Until then, TrainingPeaks runners use the manual path or the calendar-export workaround.
  • Automatic plan-conflict detection — when body signals + coach prescription disagree (e.g. HRV crashed but coach has intervals), surface a structured "consider easing" prompt. Open question 1 below covers the design call.
  • Plan-import history surface — see every prior week's coach-prescribed plan vs what was executed. Lives on /plan as a longitudinal view.
  • Two-way sync — push completed workouts back to Final Surge / TrainingPeaks so the coach sees them. Likely never · Strava already does this and competing on data plumbing isn't the moat.
  • Coach-side surface — a coach-facing view of their athletes' Faff body data. Genuinely a separate product. Park.
  • Pro tier gating — open question 2 below. If Companion Mode is Pro-only, the upgrade flow needs design. If it's free, doesn't.

Open design calls.

01
When the imported plan and the body signals disagree, what happens in the Sibling?
Tyler's coach has him on 6×800s. HRV is -12 vs baseline, RHR +6, sleep 5.4h. Three options: (A) Surface body data, stay quiet on the workout — let Tyler decide. (B) Surface body data + a soft "consider easing" line that explicitly says "message Aaron." (C) Surface body data + auto-flag the workout to the coach (if 2-way sync exists). Cardinal Rule #3 (every doctrine cites a source) makes (B) and (C) load-bearing — they need a research-defended threshold for when to escalate. Current preference: (B), conservative threshold (HRV -1σ AND sleep <5.5h AND RHR +5 simultaneously), deferred to user.
02
Pro tier or free? Does Companion Mode gate behind a paywall?
Companion runners are net-net more serious than Lilian-path runners (they're already paying $150-300/mo for a coach). A natural Pro tier. But Companion Mode is also the lowest-cost-to-build path that grows the addressable market by ~25-35%. Recommendation: ship Companion Mode free in v1.1 to grow the user base; introduce Pro in v1.2+ with Pro-tier features (multi-race planning, advanced body analytics, coach-side surface). Pro is its own design pass.
03
What does Skip Today do when there's an external coach?
In v1, Skip Today triggers the plan adapter — swaps days, rebalances the week. In Companion Mode, Faff doesn't own the plan, so it can't swap days. Three options: (A) Skip just logs ("Skipped today · 0.0 mi") and does nothing else. (B) Skip logs + opens a draft message to the coach with prefilled context ("RHR +8, HRV -10, slept 5h — skipping today's tempo, ok?"). (C) Skip logs + a structured "request a swap" form Tyler sends his coach. Current preference: (B) — the draft is the helpful version; sending it is Tyler's call.
04
How does Companion Mode handle a coach who prescribes by RPE only (no pace/HR)?
Some coaches deliberately prescribe "hard 6×800, recover well" with no pace target. Faff's workout-detail row pattern assumes pace + HR + duration. Options: (A) Show empty tail values ("—") for missing fields and trust the coach. (B) Offer to overlay Faff's VDOT-derived pace as a suggestion labeled "Faff suggests" — opt-in per workout. (C) Hide the workout-detail rows entirely and show only the coach's prose. Current preference: (A) for v1.1, revisit with user data.
05
Does LTHR auto-derivation stay on in Companion Mode?
Faff's auto-LTHR (Path A · weekly recalc from 14d HR+pace data) is a body-engine number, not a plan number. It's used in the Sibling's body context and in the stat trio. If the coach prescribes HR zones explicitly, the question is: does Faff's auto-derived LTHR conflict with the coach's? Likely not — Faff's LTHR is descriptive (what the body is doing) vs the coach's is prescriptive (what the workout calls for). They live in different cells. Current resolution: auto-LTHR stays on as a body-side reading; coach's prescribed HR overrides in the workout detail rows. Surface both if they diverge by >8bpm.
06
What's the iOS tab bar look like when Tyler's not getting plan generation?
Lilian's tab bar: Today · Plan · Races · Health · Me. Companion Mode keeps all five — but /plan's content shifts substantially. Should the tab label change ("Coach" instead of "Plan")? Or stay "Plan" with the page renamed to "Plan from Coach Aaron" inside? Tab label affinity is one of the highest-stakes consistency decisions across the product. Recommendation: tab label stays Plan · the page-level header changes. Five-tab shape is preserved for muscle memory between Lilian and Tyler paths.
