CTA copy: "Go to today" — pure direction. Why show tomorrow's day instead of saying "we'll see you tomorrow": the product's whole job is "open the app, the answer is already there." Surfacing the first day on the completion screen rehearses that contract before /today even loads.

Gradient swap: new-user purple gives way to done-state green→blue. Lilian crosses the threshold from "system bootstrapping" to "system populated." Same design language; a different state.

Open design questions

  • Step 2 — minimum signal requirement? The mock shows "Continue" needing at least one tile connected (so the plan engine has *something* to read). Alternative: allow zero connections + skip straight to a stub plan, then nag for connections on /today. Which fail-mode is friendlier?
  • "Skip for now" on step 2. Currently a quiet under-link. Does Lilian-without-Strava get a softer route (a connect-later card pinned to /today) or a hard gate (can't continue without one)?
  • Race-date entry on phone. Mock shows a static "Sep 28" — the real interaction is iOS-native date picker sheet. Does the picker open inline or in a sheet from the bottom? (Native sheet is the safe bet but the fall is busy.)
  • Completion screen — does it auto-advance? Right now it has a CTA. Alternative: show for 2s then auto-route to /today. Auto-route is faster; CTA gives Lilian a beat to absorb. Recommend CTA for v1, revisit after first 50 users.
  • Goal time hint copy. Step 1 says "Goal time · optional" with em-dash placeholder. Should we hint at what happens if she leaves it blank? ("We'll calibrate after your first run") might be too much, but the silence is also a bit cold.
  • Sign-in copy. Hero says "Already have an account? Sign in". Faff's account model is Apple Sign In (per native-iOS lock). Should the link say "Sign in with Apple" upfront so Lilian knows what she's about to tap?

My read

The three-step shape is the right shape. Step 1 carries the emotional question (where are you going?) at hero scale and earns the entire onboarding's existence. Step 2 is a utility checkpoint — three tiles, status dots, no friction. Step 3 is the cheapest possible "confirm and go" — three rows, all auto-filled, one button. The whole flow is < 90 seconds for the happy path.

The risk to watch for: Step 1's "No specific race" edge has to feel like a peer of the four race options, not a fallback. The dedicated copy block on that face (Oswald headline + body) is the call — without it, "No specific race" reads as "give up." With it, it reads as "this is a real choice."

What this deck is not: the wire-up. OAuth handshakes, Apple Sign In flow, account creation, plan-engine API contract — those all happen in a later round when this direction is picked. Right now this is one shape to react to. If the shape's right, the implementation follows.
