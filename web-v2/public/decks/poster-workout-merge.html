Why this works: the gradient hero stays clean — identity / verb / numbers / phase. The band carries the prose-y execution coaching that today lives in the Sibling. One vertical object, two textures.

Risk: the dark band visually competes with the Sibling card sitting next to it — both are dark, both have bullet points. Could feel like two cards trying to be one. Probably needs the Sibling to lose its dark MiniTiles or move them down.

My read — Direction B if I had to ship today. The runner's question at this surface is "what am I doing today and how" not "where am I in the week + where's my body" (Sibling + WeekStrip already answer those). B gives PACE · HR · DISTANCE in the most direct read, and the trio replacement isn't a loss because the body context lives 12 inches to the right in the Sibling MiniTiles already.

A is the safe-and-rich answer. C is the prose-forward answer that reads like a coach handing you a card — might be the right call if we lean into "Faff sounds like a coach not a dashboard." Pick the principle, not the layout.
