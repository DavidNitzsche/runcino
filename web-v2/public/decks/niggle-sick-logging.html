FAFF · NIGGLE + SICK LOGGING · DESIGN · 2026-05-28

How the body
says no.

The resolver routes to niggle and sick, and /today already renders them. But the signal can't fire because the runner has nowhere to log it. Two states exist with no entry point. The body says no, the app stays quiet.

This deck designs the entry surface, the data we capture, and the recovery path back to running. Mockups only. Body diagram is inline SVG.

SECTION 01 · ENTRY

Where the
runner taps in.

Three plausible homes for the "log a niggle / log being sick" affordance. Primary recommendation: below the Sibling card on /today, always visible. Two fallback affordances on /profile and the readiness ring give a runner who didn't see the chip a second + third way in.

Recommend Option 1 (from /today) as the primary. It is the screen a runner opens daily; the entry must live where eyes already are. Options 2 and 3 are quieter backups, not replacements.

OPTION 1 · PRIMARY

Two chips,
under the Sibling.

The Sibling card carries the body context (sleep, HRV, RHR, load). Tucking the two log chips beneath the tile grid puts the entry exactly where the runner is already reading the body signal. Visible without being loud. Tap a chip, open the modal full-screen.

Why this surface, not the Poster: the Poster is the verb. Adding entry affordances there pollutes the hero. The Sibling is already the place where "the body" lives — adding "the body says no" buttons is the right semantic fit.

Chip styling: amber dot for niggle (mild flag), blue dot for sick (full pause). Same chip rhythm so they read as a pair, not as competing CTAs.

SECTION 02 · NIGGLE MODAL

Where, how bad,
how long.

Niggle = mild musculoskeletal flag. Runner can still train. The modal captures four things: where (body diagram + left/right radio), severity (1–10 with runner-honest anchors), status (just started / few days / weeks), and notes (optional). One screen, no swipes.

Body diagram is clinical and labelled. Front + back view side-by-side. Tap a region to select. Common run-injury hotspots (hamstring, calf, Achilles, ITB, knee, hip, plantar, shin) are softly highlighted so the runner knows the menu without us shouting it.

SECTION 03 · SICK MODAL

Symptoms,
when, fever.

Sick = systemic illness. Plan pauses entirely. The modal captures symptoms (multi-select chips), when it started, fever yes/no (with a hard "DO NOT RUN" line surfacing on yes), and notes (optional). Less clinical, more humane than the niggle modal.

Tone is quiet. No alarming red except where it matters: the fever-positive banner. The CTA is "Pause the plan" — language that frames sick-logging as a deliberate, controlled action, not a defeat.

SECTION 06 · NIGGLE RECOVERY

Daily check.
Better, same, worse, gone.

Each day the niggle modifier is active, /today opens with a quick check at the top: "How's the R quad?" with four chips: BETTER · SAME · WORSE · GONE. Tap-once interaction; result writes to the niggle's daily log and feeds back into the resolver.

GONE clears the modifier and routes back to base 4. WORSE escalates the grade by 1 and re-evaluates the adjustment. SAME holds. BETTER drops a grade after two consecutive better-days.

Persistent niggle > 7 days surfaces a deterministic "consider seeing a physio" cue, citing the medical-referral red flags from research/methodology/05-injury-return-protocols.md §1.6.

DAY 8 · PHYSIO ESCALATION
Daily check is one-tap. Four chips, one of which is the result. No notes, no modal. The check exists at the top of /today until the runner taps something — then it collapses into the Sibling and the Poster reflects the resolver's new pick.

Physio escalation threshold = 7 days. Derived from the doctrine: most overuse injuries that persist past a week despite load management benefit from clinical assessment. The cue is deterministic and one-shot — it doesn't nag past day 8.
SECTION 07 · SICK RECOVERY

Doctrine-gated
return to running.

The return-to-run gate is not "I feel better." It's three conditions, all measurable: 24h fever-free, RHR within 5 bpm of baseline, slept > 7h last night. Until all three turn green, /today shows the gate progress and keeps the plan paused.

When the gate clears, the next morning /today opens with a soft "Ready to run?" surface — an easy 30-min run prescribed. One tap confirms; the sick state clears and the plan resumes.

Why three gates, not feel. "I feel better" is unreliable — runners are biased to under-report illness when a plan is calling. The three measurable gates remove the self-talk: fever, sleep, RHR. All have wearable data feeding them; nothing requires the runner to self-diagnose.

"Run today" is the only action. Not "back to plan", not "resume training" — start small, prove the body is back, then let the plan engine pick tomorrow's workout. The next day's resolver pick proceeds normally from there.

Open design questions

  • Foot detail subview. The body diagram main grid has "foot" as a tappable region — but plantar / metatarsal / Achilles insertion need a foot-detail tap-through. Designed as out-of-scope for v1 (catch-all "foot" enum). Worth designing in v1.1.
  • Severity scale 1–10 vs 1–5. Resolver doctrine (states.md §07) uses max_grade < 4 on a 1–5 scale. The runner-facing 1–10 is more intuitive but creates a mapping step. Mapping ratio = severity / 2, rounded. Confirmed before implementation.
  • Multi-niggle UI. Spec allows ≥2 active niggles ("2 niggles in watch" suffix). Two simultaneous niggles isn't designed here — every mockup shows one. Worth a second pass.
  • "Other" symptom on Sick. Tapping it should open a text field — currently the chip is shown as a binary on/off. Decide whether "Other" is silent (just a flag) or prompts for free text.
  • Sick-gate sleep threshold. Used >7h last night as the third gate. Doctrine in research/doctrine/foundations/00b-recovery-protocols.md may pin a different threshold — confirm before locking.
  • RHR baseline source. Used "RHR within 5 bpm of baseline" — this requires a baseline value present. For runners without 14d of HRV+RHR data, the gate falls back to: fever-free 48h + slept > 7h. Designed but not mockup'd; flag for review.
  • Niggle log entry from auto-detect. Spec mentions post-v1 auto-detect for sick (HRV/RHR convergence). No equivalent for niggle. Runner-only entry is fine for v1 — flag for v1.1 if cadence-asymmetry detection lands.

My read · what to ship first

Niggle modal + Niggle-on-/today first. Niggles are the higher-frequency event — every runner has one a month. The capture flow is the bigger UX win. Sick is the safer rail (worst case, runner takes an unplanned rest day) and the doctrine + gates need a second look at the recovery thresholds before shipping.

Ship order:

  • v1 release 1: niggle entry (Option 1 chips · modal · niggle state · daily check) — covers ~80% of "the body says no" cases.
  • v1 release 2: sick entry (modal · sick state · WeekStrip dim · return gate · ready-to-run hero).
  • v1.1: foot-detail subview · multi-niggle support · readiness-ring entry (Option 3).
