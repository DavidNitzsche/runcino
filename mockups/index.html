Why grade-adjusted pacing

Running Big Sur at even pace
is how you blow up at mile 11.

Hurricane Point climbs 520 ft in 2 miles at mile 10. The rolling bluffs after the descent to Bixby Creek give you 7 climbs in the back half. Even pace means you're overcooking every climb and under-using every descent.

Runcino uses Minetti's cost-of-running curve — the same math Strava uses for GAP — to convert grade to a metabolic cost multiplier. Hold effort constant. Let the pace vary with the course. Hit the finish line with something left.

What's in the box

Everything the race needs.
Nothing the race doesn't.

GPX-aware pacing

Parse a race GPX, smooth elevation, chunk at 800m, compute Minetti-adjusted pace per segment.

Human-readable phases

Auto-groups 53 segments into 6 landmark phases: Hurricane Point, Bixby, Carmel Highlands.

WorkoutKit-native

Generates a real CustomWorkout with pace-goal IntervalSteps — same API Apple uses for Fitness+.

One JSON, frozen

The .runcino.json schema is the contract. v1.0.0 shipped end of Day 3. Same file works forever.

Offline on the Watch

No phone in the race. Custom workout syncs once, runs offline, logs to Health on the wrist.

No accounts, no cloud

Runs on localhost + a sideloaded iOS build. One runner, one race. Zero infrastructure.
