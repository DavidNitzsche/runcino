The iOS surface

Everything imports from one JSON.

The file from the web app is the source of truth. iOS renders it, Claude personalizes the morning brief, and WorkoutKit ships it to your wrist.

C
Race-morning brief

On race morning, tap the brief → paste the NOAA forecast → Claude rewrites the plan's tone and flags any pace adjustments. Stored as a brief field in the JSON, so the Watch workout doesn't need to change.

⌯ Fueling cues

5 gels timed by phase boundary, not wall clock. Anchored to terrain so they absorb when you need them most. Each becomes a 30-sec IntervalStep on the Watch with a haptic alert and "Gel 3 · water" on the screen.

◆ Landmark cues

Hand-curated for Big Sur. Hurricane Point drummers. The last climb at mile 25. Sensory anchors to the course — so you're never surprised. (New courses get generic geometry-based labels, never scraped facts.)

Post-Big Sur (M1)

HealthKit fills the fitness form automatically. Strava adds historical context. After the race, Claude writes the retrospective and calibrates your personal GAP model for next race.
