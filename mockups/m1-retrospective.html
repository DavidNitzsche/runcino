You paced it smart.
The back half cost you, not the climbs.

Hurricane Point went to plan. 10:42/mi actual against a 10:38/mi target, HR peaked at 172 which is zone 4 — exactly where even-effort pacing predicted. You didn't overdrive the climb, which is the failure mode that ends most Big Sur debuts.

The drift started at mile 17. Your actual pace slid from 8:47/mi to 8:55/mi through the bluffs. Weather log shows wind picked up to 11 mph NW at 10:40am. That's a real exposure on Highway 1's west-facing sections — the plan's +3 sec/mi crosswind adjustment absorbed half of it, but not enough.

Fueling landed right. All 6 gels taken within 0.3 mi of their anchor. HR didn't spike late, which means you weren't running on fumes.

Net: 2 minutes of the 2:14 overrun was the back-half headwind. The plan was honest; the weather exceeded the brief. For CIM in December, I'd add 5-8 sec/mi of headroom on any exposed section and trust the HR envelope you held here.
