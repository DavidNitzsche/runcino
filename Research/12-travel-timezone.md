# 12 — Travel and Time Zone Management

## Key Definitions

- **Circadian rhythm**: ~24 h endogenous cycle of physiology. Free-running period averages ~24.2 h.
- **Phase advance**: shifting the clock earlier (required for eastward travel).
- **Phase delay**: shifting the clock later (required for westward travel).
- **Phase response curve (PRC)**: graph mapping phase shift produced by a stimulus (light, melatonin) across the circadian day.
- **Tmin**: core body temperature minimum, ~2 h before habitual wake. Light before Tmin delays; light after Tmin advances.
- **Travel fatigue**: fatigue from any travel (cramped posture, dehydration, hypoxia, schedule disruption). Direction-independent.
- **Jet lag**: circadian desynchrony from crossing ≥3 time zones. Direction-dependent.
- **Zeitgeber**: external time-cue that entrains the body clock. Light is dominant; melatonin, food, exercise, social cues are secondary.

---

## Pre-Race Travel Timing

Days on-site depends on time zones crossed, race distance, and direction.

| Time zones crossed | Travel direction | Minimum days on-site before race | Recommended days on-site before race |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 (same zone) | — | Race-day morning OK for events <90 min; otherwise 1 night | 1–2 nights |
| 1–2 | Either | 1 night | 2 nights |
| 3–4 | Westward | 2 nights | 3 nights |
| 3–4 | Eastward | 3 nights | 4 nights |
| 5–7 | Westward | 4 nights | 5–6 nights |
| 5–7 | Eastward | 5 nights | 6–7 nights |
| 8–10 | Westward | 5 nights | 7+ nights |
| 8–10 | Eastward | 7 nights | 8–10 nights |
| 11–12 | Either | 7 nights | 10+ nights, or arrive late and treat as partial-shift (see below) |

### Distance modifier

- 5K / 10K / half: table as written.
- Marathon / ultra: +1 day.

### Extreme transmeridian (≥10 zones)

Two strategies: arrive early (10–14 days, full adaptation), or arrive late and **stay shifted** — keep home-zone schedule, race during home-zone performance window if local start permits.

## Time Zone Shift Calculations

### "1 day per hour" rule and exceptions

Baseline: ~1 day per zone eastward, ~0.5–1 day per zone westward. Refined rates: westward ~1.0–1.5 h/day; eastward ~0.5–1.0 h/day.

| Direction | Adaptation rate | Days to fully entrain after N zones |
|---|---|---|
| Westward | ~1.5 h/day (range 1.0–1.5) | N / 1.5 |
| Eastward | ~1.0 h/day (range 0.5–1.0) | N / 1.0 |
| Eastward, ≥10 zones | Often <0.5 h/day (anti-phase risk) | May exceed 12 days; consider partial-shift |

### Worked examples

| Route | Zones | Direction | Re-entrainment estimate |
|---|---|---|---|
| London → New York | 5 W | West | ~3.3 days |
| London → Tokyo | 8 E | East | ~8 days |
| New York → Los Angeles | 3 W | West | ~2 days |
| Los Angeles → New York | 3 E | East | ~3 days |
| Sydney → London | 10–11 W | West | ~7 days |
| London → Sydney | 10–11 E | East | partial-shift recommended |

### Exceptions

- **Anti-phase**: eastward shifts of 9–12 zones may re-entrain by phase delay ("the long way"), behaving like a 12–15 h westward shift. Reverse light/melatonin protocols.
- **Chronotype**: larks adapt faster eastward; owls faster westward.
- **Age**: re-entrainment slows after ~50.
- **Repeat travel**: experienced travelers re-entrain ~20–30 % faster.
- **Sleep debt**: pre-existing deficit lengthens recovery.

---

## East-Bound vs. West-Bound Asymmetry

Eastward is harder. The human free-running period >24 h, so the clock naturally drifts later. Eastward travel forces a phase advance against the grain.

- NBA data: eastward travel impairs shooting, rebounds, point differential, winning %. Westward shows minimal performance penalty.
- Symptom severity and duration are greater after eastward flights at all comparable zone counts.
- **Rule**: budget 1.5–2× more adjustment days eastward than westward at equivalent zone count.

**When westward is harder**: race start falling late in destination day, well past home-zone "biological night." Late-evening events post-westward deserve attention.

## Light Exposure Protocols

Light is the strongest zeitgeber. Direction depends on timing relative to Tmin (~2 h before habitual wake):

- Light **before Tmin** → phase delay (westward).
- Light **after Tmin** → phase advance (eastward).

For wake-at-07:00, Tmin is ~05:00.

| Travel direction | Goal | Light TO seek | Light TO avoid |
|---|---|---|---|
| Eastward | Phase advance | Bright light first 2–4 h after wake (destination time) | Bright light in 3 h before destination bedtime |
| Westward | Phase delay | Bright light in late afternoon / evening (destination time) | Bright light in early morning (destination time) |

### Dose

- Outdoor daylight: 10,000–100,000 lux. 30–60 min outdoors is the highest-leverage intervention.
- Indoor lightbox: 10,000 lux at face for 30 min.
- Dose-response linear 0.2–2.5 h, plateaus beyond 3 h. Brief pulses (15 s–2 min) still produce measurable shifts.

### Pre-departure pre-shift (3–4 days out)

| Direction | Wake time shift / day | Morning light | Evening light |
|---|---|---|---|
| Eastward | 1 h earlier | Seek (bright, immediately on waking) | Avoid (sunglasses outdoors after ~16:00) |
| Westward | 1 h later | Avoid (sunglasses, dim indoors) | Seek (bright, late afternoon/evening) |

## Melatonin Protocols

Melatonin PRC is ~12 h out of phase with light PRC.

- Late afternoon / early evening → phase advance (eastward).
- Early morning → phase delay (westward; rarely used).
- Destination bedtime → sleep aid + phase advance.

### Dosing

| Goal | Dose | Timing |
|---|---|---|
| Phase advance (eastward) | 0.5–3 mg | 5–7 h before destination bedtime, OR at destination bedtime, for 3–5 nights |
| Sleep onset on overnight flight | 0.5–3 mg | At target sleep time on the plane (only if it aligns with destination night) |
| Westward phase delay | 0.5 mg | On waking at destination, days 1–3 (rarely needed; light is usually enough) |
| Maintenance at destination | 0.5–1 mg | At destination bedtime, nights 1–4 |

Cochrane: 0.5 mg ≈ 5 mg for phase shifting, fewer side effects. Higher doses give stronger hypnotic effect but no greater shift; residual melatonin at wrong phase delays adaptation.

### Cautions

- Choose third-party certified product (NSF Certified for Sport, Informed Sport) for drug-tested athletes.
- No co-administration with alcohol or sedatives.
- Mistimed melatonin **delays** adaptation and causes daytime sleepiness.

## In-Flight Hydration

### Cabin environment

- Humidity 10–20 % (vs. ideal 30–60 %).
- Pressure ~6,000–8,000 ft equivalent (mild hypoxia).
- 10 h flight ≈ 2 L net fluid loss for a sedentary passenger.

### Fluid intake

| Flight length | Recommended fluid |
|---|---|
| <2 h | 250–500 mL water before + during |
| 2–6 h | 200–300 mL/h water; 1 sodium-containing drink |
| 6–10 h | 200–300 mL/h water; 1 electrolyte serving every 3–4 h |
| 10+ h | 200–300 mL/h water + electrolytes; monitor urine color (target pale straw) |

Aerospace Medical Association: ~240 mL water/hour in flight.

### Skip

- Alcohol (diuretic, sleep-fragmenting, hypoxia-worsening).
- Extra caffeine; cap in last 6–8 h before target sleep.
- Salty snacks without matched water.

---

## Travel Compression

- Graduated compression socks (15–20 mmHg ankle, gradient) reduce asymptomatic DVT risk on flights >4 h (high-certainty Cochrane evidence).
- Knee-high is sufficient. Combine with calf pumps every 30–60 min and aisle walks every 60–90 min.

### Timing

| Phase | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Pre-flight | Optional; not required at home |
| In-flight (>4 h) | 15–20 mmHg knee-high, both legs, full duration |
| Post-arrival | 1–2 h continued wear, especially after long-haul |
| Race-day morning | Skip race-morning compression unless habitual; novelty risks GI/circulation surprises |
| Return flight (post-race) | Strongly recommended; reduces post-race leg swelling and DVT risk |

## Pre/In/Post-Flight Running Adjustments

### Pre-flight (3–7 days)

- Cut volume 10–20 % 2–3 days pre-long-haul; keep brief intensity.
- Bank sleep: +60–90 min × 3 nights.
- Begin light/sleep pre-shift.
- Arrive euhydrated.

### In-flight

- Watch to destination on boarding.
- Sleep/wake match destination clock.
- Stand/walk every 60–90 min on flights >4 h.
- Calf pumps, ankle circles, hip openers every 30 min.
- Eat light, low-fat, low-fiber; meal timing on destination clock.

### Post-flight

| Day post-arrival | Workout type |
|---|---|
| Arrival day | 15–25 min easy shake-out; outdoor (light exposure); skip if arrival is at destination night |
| Day +1 | 30–45 min easy + strides; avoid hard efforts |
| Day +2 | Easy + light tempo or 4–6 short hill repeats (calibrate effort) |
| Day +3 | First quality session permissible if alertness restored |
| Day +4 onward | Normal taper schedule resumes |

For ≥6 zones eastward, defer quality until day +3 or +4. Eastward travel suppresses peak power ~3–5 % for several days.

## Plane Sleep vs. Arrival Sleep

Rule: **align plane sleep with destination night, not origin night.**

| Departure → Arrival pattern | Sleep on plane? | Sleep on arrival? |
|---|---|---|
| Day departure, evening arrival (eastward) | Short nap only (<90 min) early flight | Yes — full night at destination bedtime |
| Evening departure, morning arrival (eastward overnight) | Yes — sleep aggressively, target 6–8 h | Stay awake until destination bedtime |
| Morning departure, afternoon arrival (westward) | Avoid sleeping | Stay awake to destination bedtime |
| Evening departure, late-night arrival (westward) | Sleep on plane | Sleep on arrival |

### Plane sleep tools

- Eye mask, earplugs or noise-canceling headphones, neck pillow.
- Compression socks (reduces leg-swelling restlessness).
- 0.5–3 mg melatonin only if aligned with destination night.
- Window seat for light/posture control.

### Arrival nap rule

Naps >30 min outside destination late-afternoon delay re-entrainment. If needed: cap 20–30 min, between 13:00–15:00 local.

## Race-Day: In-Zone vs. Shifted

### Fully entrained

Standard race-day in destination time. Wake 3–4 h before start; light within 30 min of waking. Caffeine, breakfast, warm-up on local clock.

### Partially entrained

Compute **biological time** of race: local start − (zones not yet entrained) eastward, + westward.

- 6 zones east, 3 days on-site: ~3 zones unadapted. 09:00 local = ~06:00 biological. Penalty likely; extend warm-up.
- 6 zones west, 3 days on-site: ~1.5 zones unadapted. 09:00 local = ~10:30 biological. Acceptable.

Warm-up + 5–10 min if biological time <06:00 or >20:00. Caffeine dose modestly higher (within trained range) if biological time is "early."

### Stay-shifted (extreme east, short stay)

Keep home-zone sleep/light. Schedule sleep/wake so race occurs during home-zone 16:00–20:00 window. Blackout curtains, mask, controlled light.

## Driving vs. Flying

| Factor | Drive | Fly |
|---|---|---|
| Door-to-door <500 km | Often faster | Often slower |
| Hydration control | Easy | Hard |
| Hypoxia | None | Mild |
| DVT risk | Lower | Elevated >4 h |
| Postural fatigue | High (driver) | High |
| Time-zone effect | Rare | Common |
| Nap | Passenger only | Yes |
| Own food/gear | Easy | Restricted |
| Logistics stress | Lower | Higher |

### Driving rules

- Solo drive >3 h race-morning: avoid; split with hotel night before.
- Solo drive 1–3 h race-morning: OK; arrival ≥90 min pre-start.
- Group drives: rotate driving so no single athlete absorbs all fatigue.
- Driver-athlete: ≤2 h/shift, break every 60–90 min, no driving last 12 h before race.
- Finish driving by destination 19:00–20:00 night before.

## Multi-Stop Travel

- **Layover**: <24 h connection, airport-only.
- **Stopover**: ≥24 h with overnight at intermediate city.

### When to add a stopover

| Scenario | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Eastward, ≥9 zones | Add 24–48 h stopover ~halfway; sleep at intermediate city |
| Westward, ≥9 zones | Stopover optional; westward adapts faster |
| Eastward, 5–8 zones, with budget | Stopover helpful but not required |
| Any direction, 1–4 zones | No stopover needed |

### Layover (no overnight)

Walk the terminal. Hydrate, eat light, get bright light only if it aligns with destination phase target. Avoid airport hotel naps unless they align with destination night.

### Multi-leg sleep plan

Plan sleep windows by **destination time**. Drift toward destination clock at each leg's meals and lights.

## Athletes' Village / Hotel Sleep Optimization

### Environment targets

| Variable | Target |
|---|---|
| Room temperature | 16–19 °C (60–67 °F) |
| Room humidity | 40–60 % |
| Light at night | <1 lux (full blackout) |
| Noise | <30 dB |
| Bedtime light wavelength | <50 lux, warm/red end |

### Hotel hacks

- Blackout curtains; binder clips or tape on gaps; cover LEDs.
- HVAC fan for white noise; supplement with portable device.
- Request high-floor, away from elevator and ice machine.
- Thermostat 16–18 °C; pre-cool room in afternoon.
- Verify curtains seal fully **before** race-eve; switch rooms if not.

### Athletes' village

- Village beds are often spartan (cardboard frames at recent Olympics). Bring a topper if rules allow.
- Communal noise spikes overnight; earplugs mandatory.
- Thin walls: white-noise device > foam earplugs alone.
- Communal dining adds GI risk; stick to familiar items.

## Bringing Sleep Environment Items

### Pack list

| Item | Sleep benefit |
|---|---|
| Own pillow (compressible) | High — cervical alignment, smell familiarity |
| Eye mask | High |
| Earplugs (foam + custom-fit) | High |
| White-noise machine or phone+speaker | Medium-high |
| Sleeping-bag liner / clean sheet | Medium (allergens, smell) |
| Travel blackout curtain (suction cups) | Medium-high (extreme latitudes, mid-summer) |
| Familiar pajamas | Low–medium |
| Familiar sleep supplement (e.g., magnesium glycinate) | Low–medium |
| Familiar-scent toothpaste/shampoo | Low (olfactory familiarity) |

**First-night effect** suppresses deep sleep ~10–20 % on night 1. Reducing unfamiliar variables compresses this penalty.

## Pre-Race Meal Availability

### Recon

- Pre-race dinner restaurant or kitchenette near hotel.
- Walkable grocery for breakfast staples.
- Hotel breakfast opens ≥3 h pre-start, or pack breakfast in.
- Room with fridge + microwave or kettle.

### Carb staples to pack

| Item | Why |
|---|---|
| Instant oatmeal packets | Race-morning, hot water only |
| Bagels / English muffins | Shelf-stable 3–5 days |
| Microwave white-rice pouches | Pre-race dinner backup |
| Pasta + jar of plain sauce | Pre-race dinner backup |
| Sports drink mix (single-serve) | Hydration/glycogen |
| Race gels/chews — exact home brand | No foreign-brand experiments race day |
| Honey or jam packets | Carb add-on |
| Pretzels / crackers | GI-friendly snack |
| Usual coffee (instant or aeropress) | Predictable caffeine |

### International rules

- "Nothing new on race day" extends to race week.
- Avoid local specialties, raw foods, unfamiliar dairy in 72 h pre-race.
- Bottled water in regions with questionable tap.

## Race-Morning Logistics

### Night-before checklist

- Walk/drive route to start; clock travel time and drop-off point.
- Confirm public-transit early-morning schedule.
- Lay out kit, bib, fuel, shoes, weather layers.
- 2 alarms on 2 devices. Charge watch and phone.
- Identify open restroom near start.
- Confirm bag-drop close and shuttle times.

### Race-morning timing template

| Time before start | Action |
|---|---|
| T-180 min | Wake; light exposure (open curtains); bathroom; weigh in |
| T-150 min | Breakfast (familiar carbs, ~1.5–2 g/kg) + caffeine if usual |
| T-120 min | Travel to start; arrive at drop-off |
| T-60 min | Bag drop, restroom (1st pass) |
| T-45 min | Easy jog warm-up (if event ≥10K, optional for marathon) |
| T-30 min | Restroom (2nd pass) |
| T-20 min | Strides, dynamic mobility |
| T-10 min | Corral; final fueling sip; layer drop |
| T-0 | Start |

### Buffer rules

- +15 min per unknown (parking, transit transfer, security).
- +30 min for point-to-point shuttle uncertainty.
- Study road-closure map race-eve; closures often block hotel egress.

## Returning Home

### Return-flight timing

| Race distance | Earliest flight after finish |
|---|---|
| 5K / 10K | Same day, ≥4 h after finish |
| Half marathon | Same day ≥6 h, ideally next morning |
| Marathon | 24+ h; avoid same-day |
| Ultra | 48+ h |

Same-day post-marathon flight stacks swelling, DVT risk, and travel-fatigue onto race-fatigue.

### Return-flight protocol

- Compression socks full duration (strongest case of the trip).
- Continue rehydration: 200–300 mL/h water + electrolytes.
- Easy carbs + protein; no alcohol; no salty fast food pre-flight.
- Walk every 30–45 min on flights >2 h post-marathon.
- Aisle seat preferred.
- Gentle ankle pumps; skip in-flight stretching extremes.

### Re-entrainment on return

Home zone is a phase shift in the **opposite direction** from outbound:

| Outbound | Return | Return is |
|---|---|---|
| Eastward | Westward home | Easier than outbound |
| Westward | Eastward home | Harder than outbound |

For eastward-outbound + westward-return (e.g., NYC → Tokyo → NYC): return is circadian-easier but lands on a post-race body. Plan 5–7 easy days on return regardless of distance, layering travel-recovery onto post-race recovery.

### Return-week training

| Day | Training |
|---|---|
| Race day | Cool-down walk only |
| +1 | Walk, light mobility |
| +2 | Walk + easy spin/swim |
| +3 | 20–30 min easy jog optional |
| +4 | Easy running resumes if alert/pain-free |
| +5–7 | Easy mileage only; no quality |
| Week +2 | Strides return; first quality after sleep/mood/HR normalize |

| Trip | Days on-site | AM light | PM light | Melatonin | Compression | Plane sleep |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Same zone, <500 km drive | 1 night | Normal | Normal | None | Optional | N/A |
