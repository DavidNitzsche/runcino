# 22 — Training Plan Templates by Distance and Level

Generic plan scaffolding the coach can adapt to any user. Paces here are written as **zones** (E, M, T, I, R) or **% effort**, never specific minutes-per-mile — actual paces come from the user's VDOT (see doc 01).

## Zone shorthand (Daniels)

| Zone | Name | Effort | Typical use |
|------|------|--------|-------------|
| E | Easy | ~59-74% VO2max, conversational | Recovery, warm-up, cooldown, base mileage |
| M | Marathon | ~75-84% VO2max | Marathon goal-pace work |
| T | Threshold | ~83-88% VO2max, "comfortably hard" | Tempo runs, cruise intervals |
| I | Interval | ~95-100% VO2max | VO2max work, 3-5 min reps |
| R | Repetition | Faster than VO2max | Speed/economy, 200-400 m reps |
| LR | Long run | Mostly E, sometimes with M or T | Endurance |
| ST | Strides | ~95% effort, 15-20 sec | Form/turnover, after easy runs |

Other terms used below:
- **GA** — General Aerobic, an easy-to-steady run, faster than recovery but still aerobic
- **MLR** — Medium-Long Run, 11-15 mi, mostly E
- **PMP / GMP** — Planned/Goal Marathon Pace
- **WU/CD** — Warm-up / Cool-down (1-3 mi E)
- **RW** — Run/walk (Galloway-style)
- **HM/MP** — Half-marathon pace / Marathon pace
- **SOS** — "Something of Substance" (Hansons term — quality session)

---

## How to read these tables

Each plan lists: total weeks, peak weekly volume, peak long run, key workout types, and a sample peak week (Mon-Sun by zone). The coach scales mileage to user fitness, swaps pace zones to user VDOT, and shifts rest days to user schedule.

## 1. 5K Plans

### 5K — Beginner

For runners who can run 30 minutes continuously and have run consistently for 4-8 weeks. Built from Higdon Novice and Mayo Clinic 7-week structures.

| Parameter | Value |

## 6. Base Building / Off-Season Plan

Generic post-race or pre-cycle plan. Lydiard-influenced with modern moderation. Goal: aerobic capacity, durability, no peak.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Duration | 8-16 weeks |
| Days/week | 5-6 |
| Peak weekly volume | 80-100% of last cycle's peak (or whatever level the runner can sustain durably) |
| Peak long run | 90 min - 2 hr (E, no MP work) |
| Key workout types | All E, strides 2x/week, fartlek 30 sec - 5 min once/week, hill strides, optional 1 weekly steady run just below T |
| Phases | Reverse periodization is fine: continuous E → introduce strides → introduce fartlek → introduce LT |

**Sample peak week:**

| Mon | Tue | Wed | Thu | Fri | Sat | Sun |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 6 mi E | 8 mi E + 8×ST | 6 mi E + hill strides 6×15 sec | 8 mi E w/ 8×1 min fartlek | Rest or 4 mi E | 6 mi E + 8×ST | 12-14 mi LR (all E) |

## 7. Maintenance Plan

For runners between cycles, holding fitness without progression. Based on minimum-effective-dose research: ~2/3 of training volume maintains VO2max for ~15 weeks if intensity is preserved.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Duration | Open-ended (4-15 wk realistically) |
| Days/week | 3-4 |
| Peak weekly volume | ~65% of last cycle's peak |
| Peak long run | 8-12 mi (or 60-90 min) |
| Key workout types | 1 quality (T, fartlek, or hills) + 1 LR + 1-2 E per week |
| Notes | Each run must vary in effort/distance — pure jogging maintenance fails to hold fitness |

**Sample maintenance week (was running 40 mpw, now ~25):**

| Mon | Tue | Wed | Thu | Fri | Sat | Sun |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Rest | 5 mi E + 6×ST | Rest or XT | WU + 20 min @ T + CD (5 mi) | Rest | 5 mi E | 10 mi LR (E) |

## 11. Multi-Race Year Planning

### Two Marathons (spring + fall)

Standard recreational pattern. 16-week cycle each, with 4-8 wk reset between.

| Block | Weeks | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Marathon 1 cycle | 16-18 | Specific marathon prep |
| Recovery | 2-3 | Reverse taper, easy running |
| Bridge / base | 4-8 | Aerobic re-build, no quality required |
| Marathon 2 cycle | 16-18 | Specific marathon prep |
| Off-season | 4-6 | Active recovery, optional XT focus |

A runner can hold higher mileage entering cycle 2 if recovery was structured. Research suggests one primary peak and one secondary peak per year is optimal for recreationals.

### Three Halves per Year

Each cycle 10-12 weeks with 2-week recovery + 4-week bridge between.

| Block | Weeks |
|---|---|
| HM 1 cycle | 10-12 |
| Recovery + bridge | 4-6 |
| HM 2 cycle | 8-10 (compressed; uses retained fitness) |
| Recovery + bridge | 4-6 |
| HM 3 cycle | 8-10 |

Use the second and third cycles as fitness-stacking, not from-scratch. Bridge weeks emphasize hill strides and lateral strength to keep injury risk down.

### 5K-10K Track / Road Series

Multi-race "season" pattern (Daniels Phase IV style). Race weekly or bi-weekly for 4-8 weeks after a peak.

| Race interval | Mid-week structure |
|---|---|
| 7 days | 1 short quality (Tue) + race (Sat); rest of week is E |
| 14 days | 1 quality + 1 LR + 5 days E in week 1; light week 2 + race |

## 12. Heat-Season vs. Cold-Season Planning

### Heat-Season (summer build, fall race)

Adjust volume and effort to conditions. Heat acclimation: 10-14 consecutive sessions of 60-90 min in the heat lowers HR and raises sweat rate; reverses in 2-4 wk.

| Adjustment | Detail |
|---|---|
| Long run | Run by time, not distance; expect 5-15% slower at same effort |
| Quality | Move to dawn or post-sunset; reduce I-pace volume by ~10% |
| Heat acclimation | Final 10-14 days before a hot race: deliberately train in heat 60-90 min |
| Hydration | Pre-load, mid-run electrolytes; weigh-in/weigh-out to calibrate |
| Cross-train | Heat tolerance carries from sauna or hot yoga in last 2 wk |

**Sample summer marathon peak week:**

| Mon | Tue | Wed | Thu | Fri | Sat | Sun |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Rest | Dawn: WU + 5 mi @ T + CD (9 mi, by feel) | 11 mi MLR (early AM) | 8 mi GA (treadmill if needed) | 5 mi E | 6 mi E | Pre-dawn 18 mi LR (by time, ~3 hr) |

### Cold-Season (winter build, spring race)

Cold weather is generally favorable for performance but demands surface and footing care.

| Adjustment | Detail |
|---|---|
| Quality | Move T and I to indoor track if footing dangerous |
| Long run | Layer; use treadmill for last 30-60 min if footing fails |
| Volume | Cold dry air can boost training; aerobic peak achievable |
| Strength | Indoor strength + plyo block fits well in winter |
| Snow / ice | Substitute trails for road; use traction devices; reduce I volume on slick days |

**Sample winter marathon peak week:**

| Mon | Tue | Wed | Thu | Fri | Sat | Sun |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Rest or 5 mi recovery | Indoor track: WU + 6×1 mi @ T + CD (10 mi) | 13 mi MLR (cleared roads) | 9 mi GA | 5 mi E + 8×ST | 6 mi E | 20 mi LR (treadmill bailout if storm) |

## 13. Track-Season vs. Road-Season Planning

### Track Season (5000 m / 10000 m)

Spring/early summer focus. Shorter, sharper, faster than road work.

| Phase | Weeks | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| General prep | 6-8 | Aerobic + R/strides + drills |
| Specific prep | 6-8 | T + I at race pace (95-100% VO2max) |
| Competition | 6-10 | Weekly or bi-weekly racing; light mid-week, race specificity |
| Transition | 3-4 | E running, no quality, recover |

**Sample track season peak week (5000 m specialist):**

| Mon | Tue | Wed | Thu | Fri | Sat | Sun |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 6 mi E | WU + 6×1000 m @ I (3000-5000 m pace), 2:30 jog + CD (9 mi) | 7 mi E + 6×ST | WU + 4×1 mi @ T, 1 min rest + CD (8 mi) | 5 mi E | WU + 8×200 m @ R + CD (race or 6 mi) | 10 mi E |

### Cross-Country Season

Fall focus, usually 5-10K on grass. Hill emphasis, strength, race-rich.

| Phase | Weeks | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Summer base | 8-12 | Aerobic build, hill strides, fartlek |
| Pre-season | 4 | Add T tempo, I reps |
| Competition | 6-10 | Weekly meets, mid-week T or I |
| Championship taper | 2-3 | Sharpen + reduce volume |

**Sample XC peak week:**

| Mon | Tue | Wed | Thu | Fri | Sat | Sun |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 6 mi E | WU + 6×800 m on grass @ I + CD (8 mi) | 5 mi E + hill strides | WU + 20 min @ T + CD (7 mi) | 4 mi E shakeout | XC race 5K + WU/CD (~7 mi) | 10 mi LR |

### Road Season

Fall/spring half marathons + marathons. Longer LRs, more T, less R.

| Phase | Weeks | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Base | 4-8 | Aerobic + strides + hills |
| LT | 6-8 | T-dominant; MLR; tune-up race |
| Race-specific | 3-4 | GMP/HMP work, sharpening I |
| Taper | 2-3 | Reduce volume, hold intensity |

(See marathon and HM tables above for road-season specifics.)

## 14. Comeback Plans

### Return from Short Layoff (1-2 weeks off)

Resume at previous schedule, drop intensity for first week.

| Days off | Restart approach |
|---|---|
| 1-7 days | Resume full plan; one easy day instead of first quality |
| 8-14 days | 70% of pre-layoff volume for 1 wk, 85% for wk 2, full for wk 3 |

### Return from Moderate Layoff (3-8 weeks)

Higher re-injury risk; structured criteria-based ramp.

| Week | Run frequency | Pattern |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Every other day | 20-30 min run/walk (1 min run / 1 min walk x 10-15) |
| 2 | 4 days | 25-35 min mostly continuous E running, 1 day off between |
| 3 | 4-5 days | 30-40 min E, add 4×ST on one day |
| 4 | 5 days | E runs, introduce 1 short fartlek (4×1 min) |
| 5 | 5 days | E + 1 fartlek + 1 LR (45 min) |
| 6 | 5-6 days | Approaching pre-layoff volume at ~70-80%; introduce light T effort |
| 7+ | Full | Resume normal cycle; expect 4-6 wk to feel like prior self |

Volume cap: weekly mileage ≤ 50% of lowest pre-layoff week initially; 10% rule strictly enforced.

### Return from Long Layoff (>2 months) or Injury

| Phase | Weeks | Detail |
|---|---|---|
| Walk/run reentry | 2-3 | Galloway-style 1:1 or 2:1; no continuous running yet |
| Continuous E | 3-4 | Build to 30-40 min continuous E, 4-5 days/wk |
| Aerobic base | 4-6 | E only, build to ~70% prior volume; strides last week |
| Re-introduction | 4-6 | Add 1 quality (fartlek → T → I); LR returns to 90 min |
| Full cycle | 12-16 wk away from race | Begin standard plan |

Plus: ≥2 strength sessions/week throughout; cross-training as substitute for any pain or hesitation; criteria-based progression (no symptoms, hop test, single-leg metrics) before adding load.

## 15. Plans by Goal

### Finish-Focused (any distance)

Goal is distance, not time. Volume is the lever; intensity is minimal.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Quality | Strides 1-2x/week; optional 1 short fartlek every 2 wk |
| LR | Builds to race distance (5K-HM) or 80-90% of race distance (marathon-100K) |
| Pacing | Single pace for almost all runs: E |
| Cross-training | Useful for volume without impact load |
| Walk breaks | Galloway-style RW totally fine; even encouraged for ultras |

### PR-Focused (5K through marathon)

Specific race-pace work + LT + VO2max. See Intermediate/Advanced templates above.

| Distance | Primary stimulus | Secondary |
|---|---|---|
| 5K | I + R | T |
| 10K | I + T | R |
| Half | T + GMP/HMP | I |
| Marathon | M + T | I |

### Boston Qualifier / Standards-Based

User has a specific marathon time on the calendar. Use Pfitzinger or Hansons template at runner's volume tolerance, with weekly GMP work to internalize the qualifying pace.

**2026 Boston Marathon qualifying standards (BAA):**

| Age group | Men | Women / Non-binary |
|---|---|---|
| 18-34 | 3:00:00 | 3:30:00 |
| 35-39 | 3:05:00 | 3:35:00 |
| 40-44 | 3:10:00 | 3:40:00 |
| 45-49 | 3:20:00 | 3:50:00 |
| 50-54 | 3:25:00 | 3:55:00 |
| 55-59 | 3:35:00 | 4:05:00 |
| 60-64 | 3:50:00 | 4:20:00 |
| 65-69 | 4:05:00 | 4:35:00 |
| 70-74 | 4:20:00 | 4:50:00 |
| 75-79 | 4:35:00 | 5:05:00 |
| 80+ | 4:50:00 | 5:20:00 |

Cutoff buffer: actual entry typically requires beating the standard by 2-7 minutes (2026 cycle: -5:00 standard adjustment + ~4:34 cutoff). Coach should target ≥5 min faster than published time.

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Plan template | Pfitzinger 18/55 or 18/70 (volume by tolerance) |
| Required workout | Long run with 12-16 mi at GMP, run 3+ times in cycle |
| Required workout | Tune-up half at HMP-T, 4-6 wk out, predicting BQ-pace |
| Pace check | Half-marathon time × 2 + 6-12 min should align with goal |
| Race selection | Flat, certified, weather-stable course (Chicago, Berlin, CIM, Indianapolis, Erie, etc.) |
