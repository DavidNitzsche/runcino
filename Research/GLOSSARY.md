# Glossary — Running Research Knowledge Base

This glossary defines every technical term used across the 24-doc knowledge base. Definitions are intentionally brief; for full treatment, see the linked source doc.

## A

**A race / B race / C race** — Race-priority labels used in season planning. A = peak goal race with full taper and post-race recovery; B = secondary race with partial 7–10 day taper; C = training race or hard-workout substitute, no taper. Used in: [00b-recovery-protocols.md](00b-recovery-protocols.md), [22-plan-templates.md](22-plan-templates.md).

**A-skip / B-skip / C-skip** — Canonical running drills that exaggerate the swing and pull-through phases of the gait cycle to reinforce neuromuscular coordination. A-skips drive knee up; B-skips add a forward kick; C-skips add a pawing pull. Used in: [10-mobility-warmup.md](10-mobility-warmup.md), [04-workout-vocabulary.md](04-workout-vocabulary.md).

**ACWR (Acute:Chronic Workload Ratio)** — Ratio of recent (7-day) training load to longer-term (28-day or 42-day) load. Sweet spot 0.8–1.3; ≥1.5 is associated with elevated injury risk. A heuristic, not a forecast. Used in: [15-wearable-data.md](15-wearable-data.md), [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md).

**Achilles tendinopathy** — Pain and dysfunction of the Achilles tendon, classified as mid-portion (2–6 cm above insertion, most common) or insertional (at the calcaneal attachment). Insertional cases tolerate less dorsiflexion and require floor-level rather than dropped heel raises. Used in: [05-injury-return-protocols.md](05-injury-return-protocols.md), [07-strength-programming.md](07-strength-programming.md).

**Active recovery** — Very low-intensity movement (walking, easy spinning, recovery-pace jogging) intended to maintain blood flow and acute lactate clearance without adding training stress. Used in: [00b-recovery-protocols.md](00b-recovery-protocols.md), [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md).

**Aerobic decoupling (Pa:HR)** — The percentage rise in HR (or fall in pace) over a steady aerobic effort. Decoupling >5–10% indicates incomplete aerobic durability or fatigue. Used in: [03-heart-rate-zones.md](03-heart-rate-zones.md), [15-wearable-data.md](15-wearable-data.md).

**Aerobic threshold (AeT, AT, LT1)** — The lower lactate inflection (~2 mmol/L) marking the boundary between easy and moderate intensity. Roughly the talk-test threshold. Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md), [03-heart-rate-zones.md](03-heart-rate-zones.md).

**Age grading** — A percentage score normalizing a race performance to the world record for the runner's age and sex; supports cross-age and cross-distance comparison. Used in: [02-race-time-prediction.md](02-race-time-prediction.md), [14-age-considerations.md](14-age-considerations.md).

**Air quality index (AQI)** — Composite air-pollution metric used to set training thresholds; >150 typically warrants moving runs indoors. Used in: [06-weather-adjustments.md](06-weather-adjustments.md).

**Alfredson protocol** — Twice-daily heavy eccentric heel-drop program (3×15 reps straight-knee + 3×15 bent-knee, daily for 12 weeks) for chronic mid-portion Achilles tendinopathy. Used in: [05-injury-return-protocols.md](05-injury-return-protocols.md), [07-strength-programming.md](07-strength-programming.md), [10-mobility-warmup.md](10-mobility-warmup.md).

**Altitude (LHTL, LHTH)** — Live-High-Train-Low and Live-High-Train-High protocols for hematological adaptation. LHTL (live 2000–2500 m, train 0–1200 m, ≥3 weeks, ≥20 h/d) is the preferred model; raises Hb mass ~3–7% in responders. Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md), [11-course-specific-training.md](11-course-specific-training.md), [06-weather-adjustments.md](06-weather-adjustments.md).

**Anterior pelvic tilt** — Forward rotation of the pelvis with lumbar hyperextension. Common in runners with tight hip flexors and weak glutes/abs; predisposes to low-back pain and quad-dominant gait. Used in: [21-form-corrections.md](21-form-corrections.md), [10-mobility-warmup.md](10-mobility-warmup.md).

**ATL (Acute Training Load)** — A 7-day exponentially weighted moving average of daily TSS, used as a proxy for fatigue. Used in: [15-wearable-data.md](15-wearable-data.md).

## B

**Banister TRIMP** — See **TRIMP**.

**Base / Build / Peak / Specific phase** — Sequential training phases in linear periodization: base = high-volume aerobic; build = mixed aerobic + introduction of intensity; specific = race-specific work; peak = race-pace polish prior to taper. Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md), [22-plan-templates.md](22-plan-templates.md), [07-strength-programming.md](07-strength-programming.md).

**Beetroot juice / nitrate** — Inorganic nitrate supplement (400–800 mg, 2–3 h pre-race) that lowers oxygen cost of submaximal running by ~1–3%. Better response in non-elite endurance athletes; ~15–20% are non-responders. Used in: [18-fueling-products.md](18-fueling-products.md).

**Beta-alanine** — Amino-acid precursor of muscle carnosine; chronically loaded over weeks for buffering benefit on efforts ~1–4 minutes. Mentioned (not deeply developed) as part of supplement stack in: [RESEARCH_TASKS.md](RESEARCH_TASKS.md).

**Block periodization (Issurin)** — Concentrated 2–4-week mesocycles, each loading one or two abilities heavily, sequenced so residual effects from prior blocks support the next. Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md), [22-plan-templates.md](22-plan-templates.md).

**Boston Qualifier (BQ)** — A marathon time meeting the age- and sex-graded standard required to register for the Boston Marathon. Used in: [22-plan-templates.md](22-plan-templates.md).

**BSI (Bone Stress Injury) / stress fracture / stress reaction** — Skeletal overuse continuum, from MRI-positive periosteal edema (reaction) to discrete cortical fracture line. Common sites: tibia, fibula, metatarsals, navicular, femoral neck. Used in: [05-injury-return-protocols.md](05-injury-return-protocols.md), [13-sex-specific-training.md](13-sex-specific-training.md), [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md).

## C

**Cadence (steps per minute, SPM, step rate)** — Number of foot-strikes per minute. Self-selected ~170–185 for most runners; rises with pace. Increasing 5–10% above self-selected reduces vertical loading without economy cost in most. Used in: [16-form-biomechanics.md](16-form-biomechanics.md), [21-form-corrections.md](21-form-corrections.md), [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md).

**Caffeine** — Ergogenic stimulant; effective dose 3–6 mg/kg, 30–60 min pre-race. Used in: [18-fueling-products.md](18-fueling-products.md), [08-pacing-and-race-week.md](08-pacing-and-race-week.md).

**Canova / Italian school** — Renato Canova's framework progressing from general → fundamental → specific, with workouts lengthening and quickening to resemble the race. Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md), [04-workout-vocabulary.md](04-workout-vocabulary.md).

**Canova special block** — Two quality sessions in one day (AM + PM), totaling 30–50 km combined; used as a peak-stress mesocycle stimulus every 3–4 weeks during the specific period. Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md), [04-workout-vocabulary.md](04-workout-vocabulary.md).

**Capillary density** — Number of capillaries per muscle fiber; gates O2 and substrate delivery and CO2/lactate clearance. Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md), [04-workout-vocabulary.md](04-workout-vocabulary.md).

**Carb load** — Pre-race elevation of muscle/liver glycogen via 36–48 h of 10–12 g carb/kg/d, low-fiber, high-starch intake. Expect +1–2 kg from glycogen + bound water. Indicated for races >90 min. Used in: [08-pacing-and-race-week.md](08-pacing-and-race-week.md), [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md), [18-fueling-products.md](18-fueling-products.md).

**Cardiac drift** — Progressive rise in HR at constant pace, driven by plasma loss and rising core temperature; typical rate 3–5 bpm/hour. Used in: [03-heart-rate-zones.md](03-heart-rate-zones.md), [08-pacing-and-race-week.md](08-pacing-and-race-week.md), [15-wearable-data.md](15-wearable-data.md).

**Carbon plate / carbon-plated shoe** — Stiff embedded plate (typically full-length) inside high-stack PEBA-foam racing shoes; stiffens the metatarsophalangeal joint and lowers metabolic cost. See **Super shoe**. Used in: [17-footwear.md](17-footwear.md), [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md).

**CK (Creatine kinase)** — Skeletal-muscle enzyme released into blood after fiber damage; the standard EIMD biomarker. Peaks ~24 h post-marathon, normalizes ~144 h. Used in: [00b-recovery-protocols.md](00b-recovery-protocols.md).

**Concentric** — Muscle action in which fibers shorten under load (e.g., the upward phase of a calf raise). Used in: [07-strength-programming.md](07-strength-programming.md), [05-injury-return-protocols.md](05-injury-return-protocols.md).

**Contractile** — Pertaining to the force-generating elements of muscle (actin/myosin); contrasted with elastic/tendon contributions. Used in: [07-strength-programming.md](07-strength-programming.md), [16-form-biomechanics.md](16-form-biomechanics.md).

**Couch-to-5K** — A graded walk-run progression for beginners moving from inactivity to a continuous 5K, typically over 8–10 weeks. Used in: [22-plan-templates.md](22-plan-templates.md).

**CP (Critical Power)** — Running-power equivalent of cycling FTP; the highest power sustainable for ~30–60 min, used to anchor power-based training zones (Stryd). Used in: [15-wearable-data.md](15-wearable-data.md).

**Cross-over gait (scissoring)** — Gait pattern in which the foot lands across the body's midline. Identified on posterior video; correlates with hip drop and ITBS. Used in: [16-form-biomechanics.md](16-form-biomechanics.md), [21-form-corrections.md](21-form-corrections.md).

**Cruise intervals (Daniels)** — Threshold-pace repeats of 3–10 minutes with short jog recoveries (60–90 s); allows greater total threshold volume than continuous tempo. Used in: [04-workout-vocabulary.md](04-workout-vocabulary.md), [01-pace-zones-vdot.md](01-pace-zones-vdot.md).

**CSS (Critical Swim Speed)** — Swimming analogue of Critical Power; mentioned only in passing in cross-training context. Not deeply developed in any doc.

**CTL (Chronic Training Load)** — A 42-day exponentially weighted moving average of daily TSS, proxy for fitness in the Performance Management Chart model. Used in: [15-wearable-data.md](15-wearable-data.md).

**Cutback week (down week, recovery week)** — Planned reduction of 20–30% in weekly volume, typically every 3–4 weeks, to dissipate fatigue while preserving fitness. Not the same as a taper. Used in: [00b-recovery-protocols.md](00b-recovery-protocols.md), [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md).

**Cutdown / progression workout** — Sequential reps run at progressively faster paces (e.g., mile cutdowns: 5K → 3K → mile pace). Used in: [04-workout-vocabulary.md](04-workout-vocabulary.md).

## D

**Daniels' E/M/T/I/R** — Jack Daniels' five-zone pace prescription: Easy, Marathon, Threshold, Interval (VO2max), Repetition (speed/economy). Used in: [01-pace-zones-vdot.md](01-pace-zones-vdot.md), [04-workout-vocabulary.md](04-workout-vocabulary.md), [22-plan-templates.md](22-plan-templates.md).

**Deload** — A planned reduction in training stress, used here as a synonym for **cutback week**. Used in: [00b-recovery-protocols.md](00b-recovery-protocols.md), [22-plan-templates.md](22-plan-templates.md).

**Dewpoint (Td)** — Temperature at which air becomes saturated; the most useful single humidity index for runners. Td >55°F begins compromising heat dissipation. Used in: [06-weather-adjustments.md](06-weather-adjustments.md), [19-hydration-electrolytes.md](19-hydration-electrolytes.md).

**DOMS (Delayed-Onset Muscle Soreness)** — Muscle soreness peaking 24–72 h post-effort, driven by eccentric muscle damage. Used in: [00b-recovery-protocols.md](00b-recovery-protocols.md), [11-course-specific-training.md](11-course-specific-training.md).

**Dorsiflexion** — Ankle motion bringing the foot upward toward the shin; restricted dorsiflexion (knee-to-wall test <10 cm) raises calf and Achilles strain. Used in: [10-mobility-warmup.md](10-mobility-warmup.md), [21-form-corrections.md](21-form-corrections.md).

**Dress rehearsal long run** — A full race-day simulation 3–6 weeks pre-race: same kit, shoes, fueling, start time, MP segments. Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md), [04-workout-vocabulary.md](04-workout-vocabulary.md), [08-pacing-and-race-week.md](08-pacing-and-race-week.md).

**Drill (running drills)** — Movement-pattern primers (A-skips, B-skips, butt kicks, high knees, carioca) used 1–2×/wk for neuromuscular coordination. Used in: [10-mobility-warmup.md](10-mobility-warmup.md), [04-workout-vocabulary.md](04-workout-vocabulary.md).

**Drop (heel-toe offset)** — Difference in stack height between heel and forefoot of a shoe (mm). Lower drop shifts load distally; transition rules apply. Used in: [17-footwear.md](17-footwear.md).

## E

**EAH (Exercise-Associated Hyponatremia)** — Plasma sodium <135 mmol/L during/after prolonged exercise, almost always dilutional (over-drinking hypotonic fluid + non-osmotic ADH). Treated with hypertonic saline; never with more hypotonic fluid. Used in: [19-hydration-electrolytes.md](19-hydration-electrolytes.md).

**Eccentric** — Muscle action in which fibers lengthen under load (e.g., the lowering phase of a heel drop, downhill running). Primary driver of EIMD. Used in: [11-course-specific-training.md](11-course-specific-training.md), [05-injury-return-protocols.md](05-injury-return-protocols.md), [07-strength-programming.md](07-strength-programming.md).

**EIMD (Exercise-Induced Muscle Damage)** — Microtrauma underlying DOMS, elevated CK/LDH, and impaired force production. Used in: [00b-recovery-protocols.md](00b-recovery-protocols.md).

**EPOC (Excess Post-exercise Oxygen Consumption)** — The elevated O2 cost in the hours after exercise; basis for Garmin/Firstbeat training-load scoring. Used in: [15-wearable-data.md](15-wearable-data.md).

**Even split** — Race execution in which the second half of distance is run in the same time as the first. Used in: [08-pacing-and-race-week.md](08-pacing-and-race-week.md).

## F

**Fartlek** — Swedish for "speed play"; unstructured or semi-structured insertions of faster running into a continuous run. Used in: [04-workout-vocabulary.md](04-workout-vocabulary.md), [00b-recovery-protocols.md](00b-recovery-protocols.md).

**Fast finish long run** — A long run with the final 10–25% run at half-marathon-to-MP effort; trains late-race resilience. Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md), [04-workout-vocabulary.md](04-workout-vocabulary.md), [02-race-time-prediction.md](02-race-time-prediction.md).

**Fat oxidation** — Metabolic use of fatty acids as fuel; rates peak at FATmax (~60–65% VO2max in trained athletes). Trainable via long, easy, sometimes fasted runs. Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md), [18-fueling-products.md](18-fueling-products.md).

**Female athlete triad** — Original three-component framework (low energy availability, menstrual dysfunction, low BMD); now subsumed under the broader RED-S model. Used in: [13-sex-specific-training.md](13-sex-specific-training.md), [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md).

**Ferritin** — Iron-storage protein and the principal screening blood marker for iron status. Runners (especially female) generally aim for >35 ng/mL; <30 warrants clinician review. Used in: [13-sex-specific-training.md](13-sex-specific-training.md), [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md).

**Fiber reduction (low-residue)** — Pre-race dietary tactic in T-1 to T-0 days reducing fiber to <10–15 g/d to minimize GI bulk. Used in: [08-pacing-and-race-week.md](08-pacing-and-race-week.md).

**Foot strike (heel/midfoot/forefoot)** — Initial-contact pattern; population is ~75% heel, ~24% midfoot, ~1% forefoot at typical paces. Footstrike alone does not predict economy or injury. Used in: [16-form-biomechanics.md](16-form-biomechanics.md), [21-form-corrections.md](21-form-corrections.md).

**Forward lean** — Whole-body lean from the ankles (correct) vs. lean from the waist (error producing low-back load and braking forces). Used in: [16-form-biomechanics.md](16-form-biomechanics.md), [21-form-corrections.md](21-form-corrections.md).

**FTP (Functional Threshold Power/Pace)** — Highest power or pace sustainable for ~30–60 min; used as the anchor for cycling and (by extension) running power zones. Used in: [03-heart-rate-zones.md](03-heart-rate-zones.md), [09-cross-training.md](09-cross-training.md), [15-wearable-data.md](15-wearable-data.md).

## G

**GAP (Grade-Adjusted Pace)** — Equivalent flat-ground pace for a given grade, computed via Minetti's energy-cost equation. Used to compare hilly and flat efforts. Used in: [01-pace-zones-vdot.md](01-pace-zones-vdot.md), [11-course-specific-training.md](11-course-specific-training.md), [06-weather-adjustments.md](06-weather-adjustments.md).

**Gait cycle** — One complete sequence from foot strike on one foot to the next strike of the same foot, divided into stance (load → push-off) and swing phases. Used in: [16-form-biomechanics.md](16-form-biomechanics.md).

**GCT (Ground Contact Time)** — Milliseconds the foot is in contact with the ground per step. Lower GCT correlates with faster running but is largely a consequence of speed, not a directly trainable target. Used in: [16-form-biomechanics.md](16-form-biomechanics.md), [15-wearable-data.md](15-wearable-data.md), [21-form-corrections.md](21-form-corrections.md).

**Glucose:fructose ratio** — Co-ingestion ratio for multiple-transportable carbohydrate fueling: 2:1 for ≤90 g/h, 1:0.8 (or 1:1) for >90 g/h, exploiting separate SGLT1 and GLUT5 transporters. Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md), [18-fueling-products.md](18-fueling-products.md).

**Glycogen** — Stored muscle/liver carbohydrate; primary fuel above ~65% VO2max. Marathon glycogen depletion is the mechanism behind "the wall." Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md), [18-fueling-products.md](18-fueling-products.md), [08-pacing-and-race-week.md](08-pacing-and-race-week.md).

**Glycogen depletion** — Reduction of muscle/liver glycogen to limiting levels; manifests as the marathon "wall" typically at 30–35 km. Used in: [08-pacing-and-race-week.md](08-pacing-and-race-week.md), [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md).

**Gut training** — Progressive exposure to high carbohydrate intake during running over 2+ weeks (Costa et al.) to upregulate intestinal transporters and reduce GI distress at race-pace fueling rates. Used in: [18-fueling-products.md](18-fueling-products.md).

## H

**Hansons MP-anchored** — Hansons' marathon method that pegs the long run at 16 mi (not 20+) and concentrates training around marathon pace, with cumulative weekly fatigue as the stimulus. Used in: [01-pace-zones-vdot.md](01-pace-zones-vdot.md), [22-plan-templates.md](22-plan-templates.md).

**HRR (Heart Rate Reserve)** — HRmax minus HRrest; the working range used by the Karvonen method to set zones. Used in: [03-heart-rate-zones.md](03-heart-rate-zones.md), [09-cross-training.md](09-cross-training.md).

**HRV (Heart Rate Variability)** — Beat-to-beat variation in R-R intervals; reflects autonomic balance. Tracked via personal trend, not absolute number. Used in: [15-wearable-data.md](15-wearable-data.md), [00b-recovery-protocols.md](00b-recovery-protocols.md), [03-heart-rate-zones.md](03-heart-rate-zones.md).

**HSR (Heavy Slow Resistance)** — Tendinopathy-rehab loading protocol using slow-tempo (3-s up, 3-s down) heavy lifts at 70–85% 1RM, 3×/wk; comparable or superior to Alfredson with lower volume and better adherence. Used in: [07-strength-programming.md](07-strength-programming.md), [05-injury-return-protocols.md](05-injury-return-protocols.md).

**Hyponatremia (EAH)** — See **EAH**.

**Hypotonic / isotonic / hypertonic** — Drink osmolality relative to plasma (~280–300 mOsm/kg). Hypotonic absorbs faster and reduces GI distress; hypertonic delays gastric emptying. Used in: [18-fueling-products.md](18-fueling-products.md), [19-hydration-electrolytes.md](19-hydration-electrolytes.md).

## I

**ITBS (Iliotibial Band Syndrome)** — Lateral knee pain at the LCL/Gerdy's tubercle attachment driven by hip-abductor weakness, narrow gait, and high running volume. Used in: [05-injury-return-protocols.md](05-injury-return-protocols.md), [21-form-corrections.md](21-form-corrections.md).

**Isometric** — Muscle action with no length change against resistance (e.g., wall-sit hold); used for in-season tendon pain management. Used in: [07-strength-programming.md](07-strength-programming.md), [05-injury-return-protocols.md](05-injury-return-protocols.md).

## K

**Karvonen method** — HR-zone calculation: Target HR = HRrest + (HRR × intensity %). Adjusts for individual fitness better than %HRmax alone. Used in: [03-heart-rate-zones.md](03-heart-rate-zones.md).

**Kick** — The final all-out acceleration in a race, typically the last 200–400 m on track or last 1–2 km on the road. Used in: [08-pacing-and-race-week.md](08-pacing-and-race-week.md), [22-plan-templates.md](22-plan-templates.md).

**Knee-to-wall test** — Weight-bearing dorsiflexion screen: distance from wall with knee touching while heel stays down. <10 cm flags restricted ankle dorsiflexion. Used in: [10-mobility-warmup.md](10-mobility-warmup.md), [21-form-corrections.md](21-form-corrections.md).

## L

**Ladder (workout)** — Interval session whose rep distances change in sequence (e.g., 400-800-1200-1600-1200-800-400). Used in: [04-workout-vocabulary.md](04-workout-vocabulary.md).

**Lactate testing** — Capillary blood-lactate measurement (typically with portable analyzers like Lactate Plus or Edge) to map intensities to mmol/L thresholds. Used in: [03-heart-rate-zones.md](03-heart-rate-zones.md), [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md).

**Lactate threshold (LT, LT2, MLSS)** — The maximal lactate steady state, ~3.5–4.5 mmol/L; the highest sustainable steady-state intensity (~30–60 min). Boundary of moderate and hard zones. Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md), [01-pace-zones-vdot.md](01-pace-zones-vdot.md), [03-heart-rate-zones.md](03-heart-rate-zones.md).

**LT1** — First lactate threshold (~2 mmol/L); aerobic threshold. Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md), [04-workout-vocabulary.md](04-workout-vocabulary.md).

**LT2** — Second lactate threshold (~4 mmol/L); maximal lactate steady state. See **Lactate threshold**. Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md), [04-workout-vocabulary.md](04-workout-vocabulary.md).

**LTHR (Lactate Threshold Heart Rate)** — Heart rate at LT2; anchor for Friel's 7-zone HR system. Estimated via 30-min TT, taking the average HR of the final 20 min. Used in: [03-heart-rate-zones.md](03-heart-rate-zones.md).

**Linear periodization** — Traditional model: long base period, then hill/strength, then anaerobic/interval, then sharpening, then taper. Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md), [22-plan-templates.md](22-plan-templates.md).

**LnRMSSD** — Natural log of RMSSD; used for HRV trend analysis because raw RMSSD is right-skewed. Used in: [15-wearable-data.md](15-wearable-data.md).

**Long run, marathon-pace** — Long run that includes a prescribed segment at goal MP within an easy bulk (e.g., 32 km with 24 km at MP). Signature of Canova/Pfitzinger schools. Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md), [04-workout-vocabulary.md](04-workout-vocabulary.md).

**Low-FODMAP** — Diet/product strategy minimizing fermentable short-chain carbohydrates that trigger GI distress; useful for runners with race-day gut issues. Used in: [18-fueling-products.md](18-fueling-products.md).

**Lydiard pyramid** — Arthur Lydiard's classic linear scheme: 100% aerobic base → hill phase → anaerobic intervals → sharpening → race. Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md), [04-workout-vocabulary.md](04-workout-vocabulary.md).

## M

**Macrocycle** — A complete training cycle, typically 4–12 months, ending in a peak race. Used in: [22-plan-templates.md](22-plan-templates.md), [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md).

**MAF (Maffetone Aerobic Function)** — Phil Maffetone's HR ceiling: 180 − age, with category adjustments. A simple aerobic-base prescription, weak in trained athletes. Used in: [03-heart-rate-zones.md](03-heart-rate-zones.md).

**MAS (Maximal Aerobic Speed)** — Lowest running speed eliciting VO2max; effectively interchangeable with vVO2max. Used in: [01-pace-zones-vdot.md](01-pace-zones-vdot.md).

**Marathon-pace long run** — See **Long run, marathon-pace**.

**Mesocycle** — A training block of typically 3–6 weeks with a coherent emphasis (e.g., threshold block, taper). Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md), [22-plan-templates.md](22-plan-templates.md).

**Michigan fartlek** — Alternation workout pioneered at U. Michigan: 1600 + 1200 + 800 + 400 at progressively faster paces, with each work bout immediately followed by a tempo (~1 km) recovery on the track infield. Used in: [04-workout-vocabulary.md](04-workout-vocabulary.md).

**Microcycle** — A 7–10-day training week within a mesocycle. Used in: [22-plan-templates.md](22-plan-templates.md), [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md).

**Mitochondrial (mitochondria)** — Organelles producing ATP via oxidative phosphorylation; density correlates with endurance. Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md).

**Mona fartlek** — Steve Moneghetti's signature 20-min fartlek: 2×90 s, 4×60 s, 4×30 s, 4×15 s, all with equal recovery. Used in: [04-workout-vocabulary.md](04-workout-vocabulary.md).

**MP (Marathon Pace)** — Current goal-marathon pace; an anchor used in Daniels and Pfitzinger zones. Used in: [01-pace-zones-vdot.md](01-pace-zones-vdot.md), [04-workout-vocabulary.md](04-workout-vocabulary.md).

**MTSS (Medial Tibial Stress Syndrome / shin splints)** — Diffuse medial-tibial pain along the soleus/posterior tibialis attachment; ranges from mild periosteal reaction to overlap with tibial BSI. Used in: [05-injury-return-protocols.md](05-injury-return-protocols.md).

## N

**Negative split** — Race execution in which the second half is run faster than the first; the dominant pattern in world-record marathons. Used in: [08-pacing-and-race-week.md](08-pacing-and-race-week.md), [11-course-specific-training.md](11-course-specific-training.md).

**Norwegian double threshold** — Two sub-LT2 sessions in one day, 2 days/week, both kept at 2.0–3.5 mmol/L lactate; popularized by the Ingebrigtsen brothers. Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md), [04-workout-vocabulary.md](04-workout-vocabulary.md).

**Norwegian singles** — Single sub-threshold session every other day for athletes who cannot do doubles; total threshold mileage 20–25% of weekly volume. Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md), [04-workout-vocabulary.md](04-workout-vocabulary.md).

**NP (Normalized Power)** — Coggan metric weighting variable-intensity efforts to equivalent steady power; basis for cycling TSS. Used in: [09-cross-training.md](09-cross-training.md), [15-wearable-data.md](15-wearable-data.md).

## O

**Overstriding** — Foot landing far ahead of the body's center of mass; produces braking forces, loud heel strikes, and high impact loading. Identified on sagittal video. Used in: [16-form-biomechanics.md](16-form-biomechanics.md), [21-form-corrections.md](21-form-corrections.md).

## P

**PB / PR (Personal Best / Personal Record)** — Fastest time an athlete has run at a given distance. Used in: [22-plan-templates.md](22-plan-templates.md), [02-race-time-prediction.md](02-race-time-prediction.md).

**PFPS (Patellofemoral Pain Syndrome / runner's knee)** — Anterior or peripatellar pain with stairs, squatting, and downhill running; typically driven by hip-abductor weakness and patellar tracking. Used in: [05-injury-return-protocols.md](05-injury-return-protocols.md), [21-form-corrections.md](21-form-corrections.md).

**Patellar tendinopathy (jumper's knee)** — Pain at the inferior pole of the patella due to overload of the patellar tendon. Used in: [05-injury-return-protocols.md](05-injury-return-protocols.md), [07-strength-programming.md](07-strength-programming.md).

**Pfitzinger zones** — Pete Pfitzinger's pace ranges: recovery, general aerobic, endurance, MP, LT, VO2max, recovery jog. Used in: [01-pace-zones-vdot.md](01-pace-zones-vdot.md), [22-plan-templates.md](22-plan-templates.md).

**Plantar fasciitis / fasciopathy** — Pain and degeneration of the plantar fascia at the medial calcaneal attachment; classic first-step morning pain. Used in: [05-injury-return-protocols.md](05-injury-return-protocols.md).

**Plyometric** — Stretch-shortening-cycle exercise (jumps, bounds, hops). Improves running economy via stiffer tendon-muscle units. Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md), [07-strength-programming.md](07-strength-programming.md).

**PMC (Performance Management Chart)** — Joe Friel/TrainingPeaks visualization combining CTL (fitness), ATL (fatigue), and TSB (form) over time. Used in: [15-wearable-data.md](15-wearable-data.md).

**Polarized / pyramidal / threshold distribution** — Three dominant training intensity distributions: polarized (~80/5/15 in Z1/Z2/Z3), pyramidal (~80/15/5), threshold-dominant (~50–70/25–45/<10). Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md).

**Positive split** — Race execution in which the second half is slower than the first; common in recreational marathons (~77%). Used in: [08-pacing-and-race-week.md](08-pacing-and-race-week.md).

**Power (running power, watts)** — Mechanical-equivalent metric estimated from accelerometer data (Stryd) or wrist motion (Garmin/Coros). Used to anchor pace-independent training zones. Used in: [15-wearable-data.md](15-wearable-data.md).

**Progression run** — A run starting at easy pace and finishing at marathon-to-threshold effort; a milder cousin of a tempo or fast-finish long run. Used in: [04-workout-vocabulary.md](04-workout-vocabulary.md).

**Pronation** — Inward roll of the foot/ankle during stance; a normal shock-attenuation motion. Modern evidence does not support matching pronation type to motion-control shoes for injury prevention. Used in: [17-footwear.md](17-footwear.md).

## R

**Race-specific phase** — See **Specific phase**.

**RAMP framework** — Warm-up structure: Raise (HR/blood flow), Activate (target muscles), Mobilize (joint range), Potentiate (sport-specific intensity). Used in: [10-mobility-warmup.md](10-mobility-warmup.md).

**RBE (Repeated Bout Effect)** — Protective adaptation from a prior eccentric (e.g., downhill) exposure that reduces damage from a subsequent identical bout. Used in: [11-course-specific-training.md](11-course-specific-training.md).

**RE (Running Economy)** — Submaximal oxygen cost at a given pace (mL O2 / kg / km). Lower = better; improvable via heavy strength and plyometrics. Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md), [07-strength-programming.md](07-strength-programming.md), [14-age-considerations.md](14-age-considerations.md).

**RED-S (Relative Energy Deficiency in Sport)** — Syndrome of impaired physiological function from prolonged low energy availability (<30 kcal/kg FFM/d); affects bone, endocrine, immune, GI, and cardiovascular systems in both sexes. Used in: [13-sex-specific-training.md](13-sex-specific-training.md), [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md).

**Reverse linear periodization** — Inverts the standard volume/intensity curve: speed/intensity early, volume later. Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md).

**Reverse taper** — Post-race progressive return of volume and intensity, mirroring a pre-race taper in reverse. Standard recovery scaffold for marathon. Used in: [00b-recovery-protocols.md](00b-recovery-protocols.md).

**RHR (Resting Heart Rate)** — Beats/minute at full rest; nocturnal-low captures are more stable than morning supine readings. Used in: [00b-recovery-protocols.md](00b-recovery-protocols.md), [03-heart-rate-zones.md](03-heart-rate-zones.md), [15-wearable-data.md](15-wearable-data.md).

**Riegel formula** — Power-law race-equivalence equation: T2 = T1 × (D2/D1)^1.06; reasonable for distances 1500 m – half marathon, but optimistic for marathon predictions from short-race input. Used in: [02-race-time-prediction.md](02-race-time-prediction.md).

**RMSSD** — Root mean square of successive R-R interval differences; the dominant time-domain HRV metric. Used in: [15-wearable-data.md](15-wearable-data.md), [00b-recovery-protocols.md](00b-recovery-protocols.md).

**RPE (Rating of Perceived Exertion)** — Subjective intensity scale, typically 1–10 (CR-10) or 6–20 (Borg). The most fatigue-sensitive intensity gauge available. Used in: [03-heart-rate-zones.md](03-heart-rate-zones.md), [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md), [08-pacing-and-race-week.md](08-pacing-and-race-week.md).

## S

**Sarcopenia** — Age-related loss of muscle mass and strength; countered by progressive resistance training. Used in: [14-age-considerations.md](14-age-considerations.md), [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md).

**Sodium bicarbonate** — Buffering supplement (0.2–0.3 g/kg, 60–90 min pre); modest performance benefit on 800 m–5K efforts. Used in: [18-fueling-products.md](18-fueling-products.md).

**Specific phase** — Final pre-taper phase with workouts that simulate race demands at 95–105% of goal race pace. Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md), [04-workout-vocabulary.md](04-workout-vocabulary.md).

**Splits** — Time recorded for a fixed segment (mile, km, lap). Used in: [08-pacing-and-race-week.md](08-pacing-and-race-week.md).

**SPM (Steps Per Minute)** — See **Cadence**.

**Stack height** — Total midsole thickness under the foot (mm); World Athletics caps road shoes at 40 mm. Used in: [17-footwear.md](17-footwear.md).

**Step rate** — See **Cadence**.

**Stride length** — Distance between successive contacts of the same foot; together with cadence, sets running speed (speed = stride length × cadence/60). Used in: [16-form-biomechanics.md](16-form-biomechanics.md), [21-form-corrections.md](21-form-corrections.md).

**Strides** — 4–8 reps of 60–100 m at near-mile-pace effort with full recovery; preserves neuromuscular function during base. Used in: [04-workout-vocabulary.md](04-workout-vocabulary.md), [10-mobility-warmup.md](10-mobility-warmup.md).

**Stryd** — Foot-pod accelerometer producing running-power data; often used to set Critical Power and power-zone training. Used in: [15-wearable-data.md](15-wearable-data.md).

**Sub-threshold** — Sustained intensity below LT2 (~2.5–3.5 mmol/L); the operating zone for Norwegian double-threshold and singles work. Used in: [04-workout-vocabulary.md](04-workout-vocabulary.md), [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md).

**Super shoe** — Carbon-plated, high-stack PEBA-foam racing shoe (e.g., Vaporfly class). Improves running economy ~4–6% on average. Used in: [17-footwear.md](17-footwear.md), [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md), [00b-recovery-protocols.md](00b-recovery-protocols.md).

**Sweat rate** — Volume of fluid lost via sweat per hour (typically 0.4–2.0 L/h running). Estimated by pre/post weighing minus fluid intake. Used in: [19-hydration-electrolytes.md](19-hydration-electrolytes.md).

**Sweat sodium** — Sodium concentration of sweat (typically 200–2000 mg/L); "salty sweaters" lose >1000 mg/L and need higher race-day intake. Used in: [19-hydration-electrolytes.md](19-hydration-electrolytes.md), [18-fueling-products.md](18-fueling-products.md).

## T

**Taper** — Pre-race reduction in volume (typically 30–60% over 1–3 weeks) with intensity preserved, to dissipate fatigue while protecting fitness. Used in: [08-pacing-and-race-week.md](08-pacing-and-race-week.md), [22-plan-templates.md](22-plan-templates.md), [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md).

**TID (Training Intensity Distribution)** — Proportion of training in low/moderate/high zones. See **Polarized / pyramidal / threshold distribution**. Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md).

**Trendelenburg sign / hip drop / pelvic drop** — Contralateral pelvis dropping during single-leg stance, reflecting hip-abductor weakness. Identified on posterior-view video. Used in: [16-form-biomechanics.md](16-form-biomechanics.md), [21-form-corrections.md](21-form-corrections.md).

**TRIMP (Training Impulse, Banister)** — Heart-rate-based dose metric: duration × intensity factor. Foundation for the Banister fitness-fatigue model. Used in: [15-wearable-data.md](15-wearable-data.md).

**TSB (Training Stress Balance)** — CTL − ATL; proxy for "form" or freshness. Used in: [15-wearable-data.md](15-wearable-data.md).

**TSS (Training Stress Score)** — Coggan's intensity-squared × duration dose metric (~100 = a one-hour all-out effort). Used in: [15-wearable-data.md](15-wearable-data.md), [09-cross-training.md](09-cross-training.md).

**Type I / Type II fibers** — Muscle fiber types: Type I (slow-twitch, oxidative, fatigue-resistant), Type IIa (fast oxidative-glycolytic), Type IIx (fast glycolytic, low endurance). Endurance training drives IIx → IIa shift. Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md).

## V

**VDOT** — Daniels' single-number fitness score derived from a recent race result; sets training paces via the Daniels' Running Formula tables. Used in: [01-pace-zones-vdot.md](01-pace-zones-vdot.md), [02-race-time-prediction.md](02-race-time-prediction.md).

**Vertical oscillation (VO)** — Vertical bounce of the torso per step (cm); typical ranges 6–10 cm. Used in: [16-form-biomechanics.md](16-form-biomechanics.md), [15-wearable-data.md](15-wearable-data.md).

**Vertical ratio (VR)** — Vertical oscillation divided by stride length, expressed as a percent; <6% is efficient, >8% suggests bouncing or overstriding. Used in: [16-form-biomechanics.md](16-form-biomechanics.md), [15-wearable-data.md](15-wearable-data.md).

**VO2max** — Maximal rate of oxygen uptake (mL O2 / kg / min); ceiling of aerobic power. Used in: [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md), [01-pace-zones-vdot.md](01-pace-zones-vdot.md), [14-age-considerations.md](14-age-considerations.md).

**vVO2max** — Running velocity that elicits VO2max; sustainable for ~6–11 minutes by trained runners. The pace anchor for Daniels' I-pace work. Used in: [01-pace-zones-vdot.md](01-pace-zones-vdot.md), [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md).

## W

**The wall** — Sudden marathon performance collapse around 30–35 km, driven primarily by glycogen depletion plus secondary CNS, thermoregulatory, and pacing factors. Used in: [08-pacing-and-race-week.md](08-pacing-and-race-week.md), [00a-distance-running-training.md](00a-distance-running-training.md).

**Walk-run** — Graded return-to-running protocol alternating prescribed walk and run intervals (e.g., 1-min run / 1-min walk × 20). Standard scaffold for post-injury return and beginner progression. Used in: [05-injury-return-protocols.md](05-injury-return-protocols.md), [22-plan-templates.md](22-plan-templates.md).

**Wave tempo** — Continuous tempo workout alternating between two paces (e.g., 1 min at 10K / 2 min at MP) without recovery. Used in: [04-workout-vocabulary.md](04-workout-vocabulary.md).

**WBGT (Wet Bulb Globe Temperature)** — Composite heat-stress index combining air temperature, humidity, radiant heat, and wind. ACSM/Korey Stringer race-cancellation thresholds use WBGT. Used in: [06-weather-adjustments.md](06-weather-adjustments.md).

## Y

**Yasso 800s** — Bart Yasso's predictor session: 10 × 800 m at "marathon-time" pace (e.g., 3:10 marathoner runs 800s in 3:10), with equal-time jog recovery. Approximate, not literal. Used in: [04-workout-vocabulary.md](04-workout-vocabulary.md), [02-race-time-prediction.md](02-race-time-prediction.md).
