Heart Rate & HealthKit — Deep Audit + Karvonen Migration

2026-05-22 · Triggered by "why is readiness 50/red — that's insane?". It was insane: the engine was calling easy aerobic runs "hard." This session traced the whole HR + HealthKit pipeline, fixed the root cause across every surface, ingested the metrics we were leaving on the table, and made HR legible.

The one-line story

Your easy runs average ~145 bpm and feel easy. The engine classified anything ≥80% of max HR (=145) as a hard effort and tanked readiness for it. With a resting HR of 48, the honest measure — Karvonen %HRR — puts 145 bpm at 73% reserve = easy aerobic. The fix routes every HR decision through one Karvonen definition. Your body was right; the math was wrong.

Why 50/red happened

MethodYour 145 bpm runVerdict
% of max HR (old)145 ÷ 181 = 80%"Hard" → −15/−10 readiness
% HRR / Karvonen (new)(145−48) ÷ 133 = 73%Easy aerobic — no penalty

%max ignores resting HR, so it overstates intensity for fit runners with a low resting HR. Research/03 §5 says HRR is the more accurate framework for exactly that case. The 0.80 × maxHr threshold was an uncited magic number that contradicted the canonical zones.

The keystone finding

Max HR reached the engine via users.max_hr → fitness-resolver (so pace zones / Z2 / VDOT signals used it) — but it never reached CoachState. So the two surfaces that read CoachState fell back to hardcoded ceilings:

  • coach-engine.ts "yesterday hard → today easy" used a hardcoded 152 bpm.
  • coach/coach.ts post-run verdicts ("ran hot", quality-over-ceiling) hardcoded hrmax = 180.

Fix: state.recovery.maxHrBpm now lands in CoachState, and both engines compute the Karvonen Threshold floor from the runner's real numbers.
