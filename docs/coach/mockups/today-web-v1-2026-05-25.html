What changes on web vs phone

The hero treatment

  • Coach gets the centerpiece. On the phone, voice is one of several blocks you scroll through. On a 1440px canvas, voice gets a 56px lead and a soft-green wash frame — it's clearly the first thing you read.
  • Reply chips stay the same width. We don't blow them up just because there's room. They're a quick check-in, not a CTA.
  • Greeting line is new. Phone gets a tiny date label; desktop gets Morning, David. at 64px. Different physical relationship — desktop is the "starting the day" moment.

The week strip earns more space

  • Each day shows mi + type. Phone is just the number; web shows EASY / CRUISE INTERVALS / LONG / REST. At a glance you read the shape of the week, not just the volume.
  • Today is ringed in green. Same convention as phone; the larger card just makes it more emphatic.

What's new on web (and doesn't make sense on phone)

  • Micro stat strip under coach. Four small KPIs (sleep, RHR, cadence, week mileage). Each carries a tiny delta — "−0.8 vs target", "+6 vs baseline". A glance bar that doesn't fit on a phone without crowding.
  • RHR gets its own card. The LLM mentioned it in the voice ("watching it through today and tomorrow") and the data is real — 54 vs 48 baseline. Web has room to give it a card; phone keeps it inside the coach paragraph.

What stays identical to phone

  • Color palette · type system · card primitives · coach voice doctrine
  • Every relevant card carries a coach_note line below its data
  • HRmax + RHR are not asked as profile gaps — derived from health_samples
  • Height gap card stands in for cadence prescription until height is in
