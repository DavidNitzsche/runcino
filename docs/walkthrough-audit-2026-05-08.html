5. The deeper gap — plan vs data

Runner's framing: "Data is surfacing, I can see things. But the most important thing is what should I do, how should I do it, when should I do it, and for how long. Doing this will get me ready for the next race."

The app shows data about training. The runner wants to see the plan of training. Different things.

The engine already computes ~80% of what's needed:

  • coachDaily(state).today — full daily prescription · ✅ rendered well
  • coachDaily(state).weekShape — 7 days with type/distance/pace/HR · 🟡 rendered as a chip strip, not a plan
  • coachDaily(state).next30Days — 30 day-by-day prescriptions · 🟡 rendered as 30 colored squares
  • decidePhase(state) — current phase · 🟡 shown as a chip, not a chapter heading
  • MULTI_RACE_CADENCE + race calendar · 🟡 timeline shows races, not training between

So the answer to "what am I doing this week?" is computed but rendered as 7 small letters on a chip strip. The answer to "what am I doing the next 4 weeks?" is computed but rendered as 30 colored squares. The redesign below fills that gap.

6. /training redesign spec

Each section answers a different temporal question. TODAY (specific + voice) → THIS WEEK (the rhythm) → NEXT 4 WEEKS (the arc) → BUILD CURVE (the long view) → QUALITY DAY GRID (the pattern).

/training (redesigned) ├── HEADER · "TRAINING · DAILY BRIEFING" │ ├── ① TODAY HERO (existing DailyBriefing — kept + strengthened) │ FRIDAY · RECOVERY · 100 days to AFC │ REST DAY (or override-aware copy when runner ran) │ Voice paragraph │ GOAL RACE sidebar │ ↑ Drop the chip-strip "THIS WEEK" + "NEXT UP" from underneath │ ├── ② DAILY FEEDBACK (existing — kept) │ ├── ③ THIS WEEK · NEW │ Header: "Mon May 4 — Sun May 10 · 14 mi · 1 quality · 1 long" │ Theme: "Recovery week — volume drop is by design (post-race window)" │ Grid: 7 day-cards, today highlighted │ Each card: day + date · workout label · miles + est min · │ pace target band · HR zone · 1-line description · │ done-mark for past days · click → /workout/[date] │ "WHY THIS WEEK?" expansion (collapsed by default) │ ├── ④ NEXT 4 WEEKS · NEW │ 4 week-cards in a row, collapsed by default │ Each: week label · theme · total mi (Δ vs prior) · quality count · │ long run distance · phase chip · cutback flag if applicable │ Phase strip painted across the row (BASE/BUILD/PEAK/TAPER) │ Click any week → expand inline to show 7 days │ "WHY THESE 4 WEEKS?" expansion │ ├── ⑤ BUILD CURVE (existing — kept, smaller header, repositioned) │ The long view: 12 weeks back, 8 forward │ Phase shading + 110% ceiling + race-week marker │ Future-week tint upgraded so projections are visible (fix #14) │ ├── ⑥ QUALITY DAY GRID (existing — kept at bottom, retrospective) │ 12 weeks × 7 days heatmap of what landed when │ └── REMOVED: RecentWeeksTile (redundant with build curve)

Day-card design

Workout day card

  • Day-of-week label — TUE
  • Date number — May 5
  • Workout type — color-coded chip (THRESHOLD)
  • Distance + duration — "8 mi · ~58 min"
  • Pace target — "6:30/mi" with band
  • HR zone — "Z4 · 140-158"
  • One-line description
  • Done-mark + actual on past days
  • Click → /workout/[date]

Week-card design (NEXT 4 WEEKS)

  • Week label — "Week of May 11" + "+1 wk"
  • Theme — "Specificity build · MP intro"
  • Total mi — "32 mi (+4 vs prior)"
  • Quality count — "2 quality"
  • Long run — "16 mi"
  • Phase chip — color-coded (BUILD/PEAK/etc)
  • Cutback flag — when applicable
  • Click → expand inline 7-day breakdown

The principle behind the redesign

The dashboard answers "how am I doing?" — VDOT, weekly miles, recovery score, training pulse, fun stats.

The redesigned /training page answers "what should I do?" — today, this week, the next 4 weeks, the long arc.

Today the runner is being asked to derive the second answer from the first. The redesign closes that gap.
