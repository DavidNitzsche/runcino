Watch · OLED orange tuning · halation test

Brand orange against pure black on OLED · does it glow appropriately or cause halation/glare?
← back to index
The OLED-orange problem. Pure-black backgrounds on OLED cause halation around bright saturated colors — the orange appears to "bloom" outside its boundaries, creating a visual glare effect that's worse outdoors and worse at dawn/dusk light levels.

Brand orange is #E85D26 on web · this is the sRGB value tuned for backlit LCD panels. On Apple Watch OLED at 100% brightness in low ambient, the same hex appears noticeably more saturated and "loud." A slightly desaturated variant gives the same brand identity with less halation.

This page is a visual-only test — final tuning must be verified on a physical Apple Watch under three light conditions: indoor, outdoor sunlight, outdoor dawn.

Web design (LCD-tuned): #E85D26 · 100% saturation in web tokens. Looks crisp on web at all sizes.
OLED-tuned candidate (dark mode watch): #DC5F2E · 96% saturation. Same hue, slightly desaturated. Recommended starting point for SwiftUI dark-mode asset.
Fall-back if even DC5F2E halates: #D06239 · 92%. Still recognizably "Faff orange" at a glance.

Open issue — requires v1 device testing:
  • Verify on physical Apple Watch (41mm or 49mm) at 100% brightness in indoor light · does #DC5F2E halate?
  • Verify on Apple Watch outdoors in direct sunlight · does the dark mode become readable, or does glare dominate?
  • Verify in dawn/dusk · the worst halation conditions (low ambient + high screen brightness)
  • If halation is severe at 96%, drop to 92% (#D06239) and re-test
  • If still severe at 92%, the design needs a different approach: thinner orange (outline rather than fill) or smaller orange area on the screen
This page locks the candidate range. Physical device testing locks the final value.
