Edge state · Low battery (watch · mid-workout)

Battery banner appears mid-workout · doesn't pause · workout saves locally · two severity tiers + low-power fallback screen
← back to index
Design rule · the watch never interrupts a workout. Low battery is information; the runner decides what to do. The workout continues recording locally and saves even if the watch dies mid-rep — HKLiveWorkoutBuilder persists samples to local storage every few seconds. The banner exists to give the runner agency, not to stop them.

What the banner says · V6 verified:
Battery low · 15%
Workout saving locally. Pause to swap watches?
Why this phrasing:
  • State the fact ("Battery low · 15%") · impersonal observation
  • State the safeguard ("Workout saving locally") · removes panic, gives the runner permission to keep going
  • Offer the option, don't push it ("Pause to swap watches?") · the question mark signals optionality without being warm or pleading
Haptic: Single soft pulse at the moment the banner appears · then silent · no nagging haptics during work intervals.
Dismiss: Tap to dismiss · banner stays dismissed for current workout unless battery drops another tier.

Low-power mode behavior:
  • Display drops to AOD-style minimal rendering · pure black bg, white numerals, no animations
  • HR sampling drops from 1Hz to 0.2Hz (every 5 seconds)
  • GPS sampling drops from 1Hz to 0.5Hz
  • Cadence + altitude recording paused
  • Haptics suppressed (drift alerts disabled · saves ~3% per hour)
  • Workout still records · just at lower fidelity · the summary marks the affected reps as "recorded in low-power mode"
Tradeoff is explicit: the prompt says "Saves ~30min of recording" so the runner knows the cost · they choose informed.
What this design refuses to do:
  • Pause the workout automatically · the runner is mid-effort, that's not the watch's call
  • End the workout early · would lose data the runner earned
  • Show a modal that blocks the screen · the pace number stays primary, banner is overlay-only
  • Use "Critical" or panic language · the workout saves locally, panic isn't warranted
