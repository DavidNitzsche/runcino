V6 voice rules (from web docs/V6.md, mirrored to native):
  • Warm second-person · when speaking to the runner's body or state ("Ease down. Nice work." · "Take it slow today.")
  • Impersonal observation · when reporting data ("3 threshold workouts trended faster" · "RHR up 4 bpm vs 7-day baseline")
  • "We"/"Our" · when the coach is making a verdict ("What would change our mind:" · "Our read: ahead by ~30s")
  • No emoji, no exclamation marks · the watch is a stoic interface. No "Great job! 🎉"
  • Avoid generic encouragement · "Keep going!" is banned. Specifics or silence.
  • Numbers don't get adjectives · "6:31 target" not "ambitious 6:31 target"

Documented deviations from V6 web voice (none currently)
Native does not deviate from V6 web voice at this round. Every prose moment on watch and every prose moment on iPhone maps cleanly to one of the three V6 categories (warm second-person · impersonal observation · coach verdict). If a deviation is identified during round 3 annotation, it gets logged here with the reason and the proposed fix.
Voice carries across surfaces by design. The "Last time (4/15)…" coach line appears identically on the iPhone Today screen and on the watch idle screen. This is intentional · the runner experiences one coach with one voice, regardless of which surface surfaces the cue. The same canonical V3 trajectory state ("On track") and the same canonical falsifier prefix ("What would change our mind:") appear on the iPhone race detail screen and on the web race hero · web ↔ iPhone parity is enforced.
Banned patterns checked for · none found:
  • No exclamation marks anywhere · ✓
  • No emoji except utility marks (✓, ⚠, ▶, etc. · functional iconography, not emotional decoration)
  • No "Great job", "Keep going", "Almost there" generics · ✓
  • No adjectives on numbers ("ambitious 6:31") · ✓
  • No second-person warmth on pure data ("Your current pace") · we say "CURRENT · MIN/MI" instead · ✓
