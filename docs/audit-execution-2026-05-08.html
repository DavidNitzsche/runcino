What you'll feel when you open the app

  • Dashboard is noticeably lighter. One mode card (not two), one home for the runner's name (not big and shouty), one home for the goal race, one home for the run, one home for the voice. Yoga widget gone.
  • Readiness banner is honest. When you're 51% easy in a 90%-target recovery window, it says EASE OFF (yellow) with the "BUT —" caveat, not READY (green) with a smile.
  • /training answers "what should I do, how, when, for how long, why". THIS WEEK is 7 full day-cards; NEXT 4 WEEKS is theme-tagged week cards click-to-expand; Build curve sits below; Quality day grid at the bottom.
  • Course implications make sense. "Steepest climb 8.1% at mile 0.8" — not 1282%.
  • /workout/[date] knows you ran on a rest day. Hero says "RAN ON REST DAY · Lunch Run · 7.4 mi · 7:42/mi" instead of "REST DAY · 0.0 mi".
  • /season has a real "your priorities aren't realistic" callout at the top — "7 A-races flagged in 12 months (max 2). Every race is marked priority A. That makes none of them priority A."
  • Past-race detail is focused on post-race retrospect. Nerd plots (gradient distribution / heading rose / trackpoint spacing) only render pre-race when they're actually useful.
  • Build curve has visible future projections — dashed-bordered phase-tinted bars project the runner's last-4-week average through the macro arc.
