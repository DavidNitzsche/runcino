The mechanism — why the skin is fully revertable

You asked that the dark theme stay reachable. It does — the paper skin is a swap layer on both platforms, not a rewrite. No view hardcodes a color; every surface reads a token. Reverting the whole app is a one-line-per-token flip plus a color-scheme line.

Web    →  <html data-skin="paper">   ✕ remove attr  → dark :root wins
iOS    →  static let bg = Paper.bg   →  = Dark.bg   (×16 aliases)
          FaffApp .preferredColorScheme(.light) → .dark

That's the entire revert surface. Gradients and zone colors never branched, so they need no change either way.
