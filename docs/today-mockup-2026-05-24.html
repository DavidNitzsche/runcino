What's different

  • Coach voice IS the page. The opener "GREAT LONG RUN TONIGHT." is in Bebas Neue 38px — the role the giant number plays on a watch face. Below it, three short coach paragraphs do the work that 6 separate cards used to.
  • One observation per run. Cadence-low called out + contextualized, not buried in a 6-tile RUNNING FORM grid. Other metrics (vert osc, ground contact, stride length) hide on Splits + Route page where they belong.
  • Inline feedback chips. "Let me know how it feels" — coach's actual ask becomes three tap targets right where it's read. No modal, no navigation, loop closed.
  • Run recap is typography. 11.1 / 8:50 / 1:38 — three numbers, three colors (blue/green/white), 54px Bebas. Same DNA as watch face wf-15-summary. No tiles, no borders, no labels for labels.
  • Week strip is sparse. Mileage per day + completion dot + type tag. Today highlighted in green. 7 days, glanceable in under a second. No "View Full Schedule →" CTA — the link is the day.
  • No banners. Anywhere. Plan adaptation is in the coach voice. Readiness is in the coach voice. Mode overrides are in the coach voice. The only standalone surfaces are the run (evidence) and the week (forward look).
  • Dark canvas, 3-color discipline. Green = good/done. White = neutral fact. Blue = distance/forward. Amber = warning (the "warm" weather chip). Used sparingly, never decoratively.
