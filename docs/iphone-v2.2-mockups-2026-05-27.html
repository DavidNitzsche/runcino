The drill-down. Drag up, depth appears.

01 · hero compresses
From 50% to 12% of screen. Verb shrinks to 32px, stats inline. The gradient stays — color anchors what state you're in even at compressed size.
02 · suggested route
"Regent's Canal loop · 5.9 mi · flat" — coach picks based on workout type. Easy days get flat shaded routes; tempo days get tracks; long days get loops with right total elevation. Atlas concept from v1, contained.
03 · HR zone band
5-zone bar with Z2 visually highlighted. Cap 148 written into the corner. Replaces "what does Z2 mean for me?" with an answer: Z2 = 132–148.
04 · 14-day HRV sparkline
Trend beats spot reading. The downward arc is what tells you to be honest about today, not just the single number 38. Trailing 14d sparkline with start/end labels.
05 · phase progress ticks
5 segments — BASE · BUILD · PEAK · TAPER · RACE. Current position pin-marked at 75% of BUILD. Where you are in the cycle, glanceable.
06 · vitals grid · existing
Carried over from v2.1's chip strip — but here as small tiles. Same data, denser format because you're already in depth mode.
07 · treadmill toggle
Inline toggle. Flip it and the panel re-renders with speed × incline pair instead of pace × HR. Surfaces in the expanded view because it's a power-user move — but accessible from today's home in two taps.
Why the slide matters. Every piece of content above costs zero new data work — it's already in the system. The slide is the surface that lets us show more without making the home feel busier. Default snap stays clean; depth lives on demand.

The honest decision moment.

01 · new state · MISSED
12th day-state in the resolver. Detected when plan.scheduled_workout exists for prior day and actual.run is null. Yellow-amber gradient — not red. This isn't punishment, it's a choice.
02 · two-button hero
First time the hero panel renders a choice instead of a verb. CATCH UP or MOVE ON. Default-recommended button is filled white (the pref move); alternative is outlined. One tap commits.
03 · plan-aware default
Recommendation isn't fixed — it's keyed off load + quality-hit-rate this block. If load is already high → recommend MOVE ON. If quality streak is weak → recommend CATCH UP. Resolver clause, ~6 lines of code.
04 · consequence preview
Bottom panel shows what the recommended choice actually does — the plan after. Sunday's long protected. Build week intact. Removes the "wait, what just happened to my plan?" panic.
05 · load-impact reasoning
The "WHY MOVE ON" callout shows the math: "6 of 7 quality hit. Catching up pushes load to 1.48." Honest, citable, no LLM. Data-driven advice rendered as text.
06 · alternative collapsed
The non-recommended choice stays visible but secondary — collapsed card with the consequence. Tap to expand if curious. Defaults are loud; alternatives are quiet.
07 · week strip · missed indicator
Tuesday now shows 6×800 in strikethrough amber. The week-strip is the only place this stays visible after the choice — a quiet artifact of the decision.
Why this matters. Real-world training is full of missed workouts. v2.1 quietly lied about them. v2.2 owns them. The MISSED state alone is probably the most retention-relevant move in the entire spec — it's the screen that turns "shit, I broke the plan" into "okay, here's what we do."
where this lands

this is what sprint 02 ships.

Three use cases validate the v2.2 spec. Sprint 01 ships the v2.1-equivalent (spine + 7 gradients + base verbs). Sprint 02 ships these three — prescription line, expanded view, MISSED state — plus the other 5 new day-states and the modifier suffix system.

The prescription line + HRV verdict label + race chip with goal delta are three lines of code in the verb-rendering layer. The expanded view is one new SwiftUI view that composes existing components. The MISSED state is one new clause in the resolver plus the two-button choice component (reusable for future "user picks once" moments).
