make it
a fucking app.

Right now the iPhone surface is the web app, crammed. This deck takes the opposite swing: what if Faff was a native app first? A daily habitat — coach in your pocket, race weeks that take over, a live spectator mode for your people, and an offline-first central store so every screen feels instant. Many directions, many variants, sized hero-scale and built on the canonical brand tokens. Pick what's slap. Burn the rest.

first principles

the 5 things faff has to answer.

Every direction in this deck is graded against these. If you can't get the answer in under 3 seconds of glancing at the home screen, the design loses. Numbered in the order you actually need them on a real day.
01
I'm running this today.
Workout type, distance, target pace/HR, when. Visible without tapping.
02
My health is like this today.
Readiness, sleep, HRV, RHR, niggles, ACWR. One readout. Honest.
03
Coach says this.
The voice. Why today is what it is. State-aware, not generic.
04
My race is in N weeks — am I on track?
Trajectory line, gap to goal, what's still wobbling. Confidence at a glance.
05
Race-day, I open here.
Bib, corral, transport, weather, drop bag, who's tracking. The 5am screen.
design pillars

four bets.

the foundations every direction rides on
01
Glance-first

Designed to be looked at, not navigated.

The web app is information. The iPhone app is a habitat. The home screen answers the 5 questions before your thumb moves. Everything deeper is a tap, not a tour.

02
Coach is the soul

The voice is the product. Not a panel.

Faff's coach engine (24 topic kinds, mode-aware, closed-loop) is the central nervous system. The app surfaces are just where the voice lands. Every screen has a coach beat.

03
Central store

Offline-first. Sync silently. Always fresh.

One source of truth on the server. iPhone keeps a local mirror in SwiftData. Background sync + push invalidation. Open the app underground at 6am — it's already today's data.

04
Spectator

Running alone, watched together.

The flagship. Your people see your live pace, predicted finish, and send emoji bumps that hit your watch mid-effort. Race-day magic, training-week glue.

pick a personality.

every direction shows its TODAY hero — the single most-opened screen
Twelve distinct app personalities. Each one gets a hero TODAY screen — the daily home — designed to make its argument in one frame. Some shout. Some whisper. Some are bets, some are safe. Scoring chips show which of the 5 essentials it nails outright versus which it dodges.

Atlas · the route is the headline

Map proposes the route — not just records it. Coach picks where to run based on what kind of run today is (flat for easy, hills for tempo, soft surface for recovery). Numbers below the map. Best for: urban runners with route variety, anyone tired of running the same loop.

In-race · pocket view · full-bleed
One surface, four states. The Race tab quietly evolves. T−14d it's about confidence (trajectory + on-track). T−72h it adds the logistics block (bib, transport, weather, fueling, drop bag, spectator panel). In-race it goes full-bleed: live pace, predicted finish, bumps from your people coming in. After the line: post-race debrief takes over (not shown — the run-detail mockup deck handles it).
flagship · pillar 04

spectator
mode.

Running is lonely. Your people make it not lonely. Spectator mode lets family, friends, and other Faff runners follow your training and races live — see your pace, your predicted finish, your route on a map — and send you emoji bumps that land on your watch mid-effort. No social feed bullshit. No likes. No comments. Just they're here, they see you, here's a fist-bump. A 4-mile training run on a wet Tuesday becomes a thing your sister can drop a 💪 into. Race day becomes shared.
How it works: Spectators don't need a Faff account — they get a link. Each runner has a public profile + private "share circle." Live tracking auto-activates from GPS start. Bumps are pre-baked emojis + 12 stock cheers ("GO ON SON" / "killin it" / "I love you"). Watch surfaces them as a 4-second haptic + visual takeover. Stops automatically when the run ends. Privacy default is private — opt-in.

pillar 03 · the central store

one truth.
three surfaces.

Right now the iPhone is mostly stateless. Each tab calls /api/... on appear, caches the raw bytes, renders. That's why every tab change costs a network round-trip and every cold start feels slow. The fix: a real local store on the phone, treated as the source of truth for what the UI renders, kept in sync with the server in the background.
DATA FLOW
SYNC STRATEGY
Background by default.
Push to bust.
  • BG sync on app foreground + every 15min via BGAppRefreshTask
  • Server emits silent push (content-available) on briefing-cache bust (new run ingest, day rollover, plan swap)
  • Phone treats push as "this surface is stale" — fetches delta, updates store, UI reacts
  • No polling. No "pull to refresh" needed.
OFFLINE-FIRST RULES
UI reads local.
Always.
  • Open the app in airplane mode — every screen renders last good state with a subtle "synced 14 min ago" tag
  • Writes (check-ins, profile edits, run notes) go to a pending queue + apply optimistically
  • Queue drains when network returns; server-conflict resolution is server-wins by default, surface to the user only on edits to user-authored content
  • Underground at 6am → today's run still on the screen
CACHED ENTITIES
Everything
the UI binds to.
  • Runner · profile, thresholds, max HR, LTHR, RHR
  • Plan · current week + 3 ahead, full days, phases
  • Run · last 30 days fully, summaries beyond
  • Race · all upcoming, last 2 completed
  • Briefing · today's per surface, cache key includes invalidation reasons
  • Vitals · 14 days of derived signals
  • Spectator graph · circle members, bump history (90 days)
WHY THIS MATTERS
Three reasons. One: instant tab switching — UI reads from a local store, not the network. Two: the coach engine already busts the briefing cache on real events (run ingested, plan changed, day rolled). The phone gets that signal as a push and updates without you doing anything — meaning the home screen is actually fresh when you open it. Three: Spectator mode needs this. Live tracking, bump delivery, watch-emitted samples — none of this works reliably without a local store + push channel. Build it once, every feature compounds.
