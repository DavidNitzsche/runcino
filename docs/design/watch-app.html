Apple Watch · the app

Run it,
don't read it.

The watch is the execution layer. Same design language as the phone, Bebas numbers and the orange/green/amber semantics, but dark for the wrist and stripped to what you can absorb in a glance at speed. The whole job: know if you're on pace right now, and feel the transitions without looking. Companion app, so the phone holds auth and pushes the workout. No coach prose here.

The primary view

Five things, in strict rank. Read top to bottom only if you have time; most glances stop at the color.

1Where you areRep 3 of 6 in amber, total elapsed top-right, and a hairline strip of the whole session above (done green, current orange). Orientation without thinking.
2Hero · paceCurrent pace, the biggest thing on the watch, color-coded. Green means on the band. You don't read it, you feel it.
3ReferenceTarget and live delta, small. There when you want the exact number, ignorable when you don't.
4Two statsHeart rate + cadence, equal weight. The unit is the label, bpm and spm, no words to crowd the glance.
5Rep progressA bar and the time left in the rep. Pairs with the haptic that fires 3 seconds before the rep ends.

G

Why it works on the wrist

01

One hero, always

Exactly one number is biggest on every screen. The glance lands in well under a second because there's nothing to choose between.

02

Color before digits

On-pace is green, drift amber, off red. You react to the color and never have to parse the number at speed.

03

Feel, don't watch

Haptics own the transitions, distinct patterns for work-ends and rest-ends, so the watch can stay at your side mid-rep.

04

Detail on demand

Splits, the session map, and controls are one swipe or one crown turn away. The default never gets busier.

05

Dark by design

Black saves OLED battery, holds up in direct sun, and dims cleanly when your wrist drops. Same language as the phone, right surface for the wrist.

06

No coaching here

The why lives on the phone, before and after. Mid-run, prose is cognitive load at exactly the wrong moment.
