01 · The gap

Most apps hand you a plan.
None of them know what today is.

A plan written twelve weeks ago does not see the heat, the bad night of sleep, or the two miles of climbing at mile ten. And the moment you start the workout, the knowledge is on your phone while the work is on your wrist. So you run, and you do clock math.

01

Static plans, living bodies

The same prescription every Tuesday, regardless of recovery, weather, or where you are in the arc.

02

Knowledge in the wrong place

The workout structure lives on the phone. The execution happens on the watch, which does not know the plan.

03

Numbers without a coach

Dashboards show HRV, pace, and load. They rarely tell you the one thing to do about it, and why.

03 · The job

Answer three questions
in two seconds.

1

What should I do today?

The prescription. The single most important answer, and the one with the largest type.

2

How am I doing it?

The body state. Recovery, readiness, the conditions you are about to run into.

3

How am I doing overall?

The trajectory. Where you sit in the arc, on track or drifting.

If a stranger looks for two seconds and cannot answer all three, the hierarchy is wrong.

08 · Voice

Coach voice, not app voice.

  • Short and direct. A note is one or two sentences, never three.
  • No hype. No "crushing it", no "let's go", no exclamation marks.
  • No emoji, no em dashes. Use periods.
  • Stats speak first. Words add context only when they add meaning.
  • Always the why. Never just the what.
  • Honest, even when it stings. "A bit hard, back off." "Recovery is the workout."
Why
Threshold day. The engine work that makes marathon pace feel like cruising. Hold the band, not the hero pace.
Focus
Comfortably hard, repeatable, never racing the reps. If rep six matches rep one, you ran it right.
Back off if
Pace drifts more than ten seconds off target for two reps running. The session is the stimulus, not the suffering.
Race week
Volume drop is intentional. Let the legs absorb the work. Day 1 of 14.

Periodization

Four phases, sequenced

Base, build, peak, taper across 16 to 24 weeks. Marathon-specific work peaks last, not VO2max. Adaptation lives in the deload.

Body signals

Readiness, honestly

HRV, resting HR, sleep, and a subjective check-in compose readiness. Each input is context-filtered for heat, race recency, and illness before it counts.
