What this unlocks

No more typing numbers
Runcino already reads them.

Fitness summary auto-fills

The goal-setting form shows HealthKit + Strava data already populated. You adjust, not enter.

HR zones per phase

Phases get target HR bands from your HealthKit history, not population defaults. HR wins if pace and HR disagree.

Weather folded into the plan

Race-week forecast pulls daily. Day-before: final pace adjustments bake in. Morning-of: you just run.

Shoe rotation suggestion

Strava tracks your shoe mileage. Runcino picks the right pair for Big Sur's terrain from what's under 300 miles.

Race-equivalent goal

Your LA 3:40 + 5 months of training extrapolates to a specific current-fitness marathon time. No more guessing.

Calendar-aware

Training runs generated around your actual calendar (travel, work blocks, family). No dumb "Saturday long run" when you're flying.

Privacy model

HealthKit — read-only permissions. Runcino never writes to Health. Requested scopes are the narrow set above, not "everything."
Strava — OAuth with the activity:read, profile:read_all scopes. Tokens live in iOS keychain. Revocable at any time from strava.com/settings/apps.
Calendar — read-only, scoped to calendars you select. Not synced anywhere, never exported.
NOAA — no auth, no account. Runcino issues an API call with your race location only.
Claude API — the only outbound call. Sends a compact numeric summary (no names, no locations beyond city-level). Cached to reduce token spend.
