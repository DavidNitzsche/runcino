What this mockup is arguing

The pivot
The timeline mockup was too abstract — too much novel data vocabulary to learn. The Strava-style top-down map is the universal language. Runners already know how to read it. So lead with the geography and the world around the route — ocean, coastline, parks, the actual road — and let the data live in the rail beside it.
The basemap matters
Dark CartoDB tiles with labels. Shows the Pacific on the left, the Santa Lucia range on the right, Highway 1 as the thread between them. This IS Big Sur. A flat line on a blank card can't say that — you need the world.
Mile markers as letters
Every mile is a lettered circle (S, A, B, C… F) like Strava. Bigger circles mark the moments that matter: start, Hurricane Point, key gels, finish. Right rail lists them in order — hover a row, the map zooms to it (interaction, not shown in static mock).
The rail replaces the phase cards
Eight rows, ordered by mile. Each one is a decision: when to eat, when to walk, when to kick. This is the race plan as a checklist, pinned to geography. The old 5 phase-cards were a summary of a summary.
Elevation lives below
Thin strip, full width, matches mile axis to the map above. Same phase colors. Hurricane peak labeled. That's all. No grade-distribution chart, no per-mile grades — those move into the full splits table further down the page.
