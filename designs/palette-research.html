How to read this.

Each palette holds a single identity — a mood + a rule for where color lives. The same three demo tiles (pace spark / recovery / race countdown) appear in every card so you can compare identically, not imagine.

  1. Base temperature (warm paper vs. cool paper vs. dark) sets the emotional floor. Warm paper reads analog/editorial; cool paper reads clinical/tech; dark reads instrument panel.
  2. Accent count ranges from one (Swiss) to two (most) to "two inks" (Riso). More than two gets loud — which is the lesson from the last iteration.
  3. Race signal should be the most distinctive color — that's the whole event. In Newsprint it's red, Topographic it's rust, Stadium it's mustard, Dusk it's copper, Riso it's pink, Swiss it's red, Terminal it's amber, Varsity it's crimson.
  4. Calendar headroom: the palette needs to color-code 4+ workout types without new hues. Solved via shade ramps of the accent (pale→mid→deep) rather than rainbow.
